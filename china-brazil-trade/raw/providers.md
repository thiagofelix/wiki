## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

China and Brazil are each other's largest trading partners, with bilateral trade reaching **USD 157–188 billion in 2024** (depending on measure). China has been Brazil's top trading partner for 15 consecutive years. In Q1 2025, **41% of China-Brazil trade was already settled in yuan/real directly**, bypassing the dollar—a structural shift that reduces costs for trade facilitators. The Brazilian e-commerce fulfillment market generated **USD 7 billion in 2024** and is forecast to reach USD 15.1 billion by 2030 (CAGR 13.7%). This document maps every major provider category relevant to a China-Brazil trade facilitation business.

### 1.3 Consolidation Services in China (Shenzhen, Yiwu, Guangzhou)

Consolidation warehouses (also called "集货仓") combine goods from multiple Chinese suppliers into a single outbound shipment, reducing costs 20–40% versus shipping separately.

#### Key Consolidation Hubs

| City | Specialization | Notes for Brazil Importers |
|---|---|---|
| **Shenzhen (Yantian port)** | Electronics, tech products, consumer goods | Direct routes to Santos; 27–45 days; South China routing hub |
| **Guangzhou (Nansha port)** | Textiles, machinery, small appliances, furniture (Foshan adjacent) | 35–45 days to Santos; BL Shipping, Betterluck Shipping based here |
| **Yiwu (Ningbo port)** | Small commodities, retail goods, accessories, garments | Major source for Brazilian resellers; active buying agent ecosystem; Goldenshiny, Yitong Logistics specialize in Brazil route |
| **Shanghai/Ningbo** | Machinery, industrial equipment, textiles | Most direct routes to Santos; 33–46 days |

#### How Consolidation Works for Brazil Importers
1. Goods from multiple Chinese suppliers shipped to consolidation warehouse
2. Inspection, relabeling, and documentation prepared
3. Cargo combined into single FCL or LCL container
4. Single customs declaration filed on departure
5. Brazil-side: single import process at port of entry

**Cost savings from consolidation vs. individual LCL:** 25–40% on freight; 30%+ when 3+ small orders merge into FCL equivalent.

**Key operators:**
- **Tonlexing International Logistics (Shenzhen):** Consolidation + quality checks for Brazil shipments
- **BL Shipping / Betterluck Shipping (Guangzhou):** Specialized China→Brazil forwarder; 18 years in market; accepts BRL via XTransfer
- **Goldenshiny Trading (Yiwu):** Yiwu buying agent with active Brazil client base; 40HQ container case study documented
- **China Gate (operates at TCP Paranaguá):** Brazil-side NVOCC that pairs with Chinese consolidators; 48-hr LCL clearance at TCP

**For a trade facilitator, the model is:**
- Establish preferred consolidation warehouse in Shenzhen (electronics) and Yiwu (general merchandise)
- Use NVOCC or LCL consolidation agreements for clients with under-FCL volumes
- Negotiate volume-based rate agreements (contract rates vs. spot rates can be 30–50% lower)

### 1.4 Customs Brokers (Despachantes Aduaneiros) in Brazil

#### Regulatory Framework
All Brazilian importers must be **registered in RADAR** (Registro e Rastreamento da Atuação dos Intervenientes Aduaneiros) — the Receita Federal's importer authorization system.

**RADAR Categories:**

| Category | Import Limit (per semester) | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Expressa** | Up to USD 50,000 | Small importers, trial shipments |
| **Limitada** | Up to USD 150,000 | SMEs with regular imports |
| **Ilimitada** | No limit | Large importers, trading companies |

*Registration is free but requires valid CNPJ, fiscal compliance, financial capacity documentation. Approval can be immediate (via e-CAC/Habilita system) or up to 10 business days. A despachante aduaneiro can assist with registration.*

#### Role of the Despachante Aduaneiro
- Files Declaração de Importação (DI) in SISCOMEX
- Represents importer before Receita Federal, ANVISA, INMETRO, MAPA (sanitary/phytosanitary)
- Manages document compliance (fatura comercial, packing list, licença de importação)
- Calculates and pays II, IPI, PIS/COFINS, ICMS, AFRMM
- Handles cargo release and coordinates with terminal operators

#### Typical Customs Brokerage Costs (per import operation)

| Fee | Amount |
|---|---|
| Despachante aduaneiro fee | BRL 450–1,500 (USD 90–300) depending on complexity |
| SISCOMEX fee | BRL 30 per operation |
| Customs brokers union contribution | BRL 160 |
| Total typical broker + fees | USD 100–300 per shipment |

*Source: [International Trade Administration / Trade.gov](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-import-tariffs)*

#### Key Brazilian Customs Brokerage Firms Specializing in China Imports

| Firm | Location | Specialization |
|---|---|---|
| **Guelcos** | São Paulo / Santos | China import consultancy; full end-to-end service |
| **De Paula Assessoria Aduaneira** | Multiple locations | China import management; Siscomex/Mantra integrated |
| **Brazinter** | São Paulo | 25+ years; São Paulo customs |
| **WM Trading** | São Paulo | Includes bonded warehouse services |
| **Grupo Serpa** | Various | RADAR registration assistance |

#### Import Tax Structure (Brazil)

| Tax | Rate | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Import Duty (II) | 10–35% (commercial) / 60% (postal/non-commercial) | CIF value |
| IPI | 0–15% | CIF + II |
| PIS | 2.1% | CIF + taxes |
| COFINS | 9.65% | CIF + taxes |
| ICMS | 17–20% (varies by state) | CIF + all federal taxes |
| AFRMM (Maritime Freight Tax) | 25% of ocean freight cost | Ocean freight value |

**Example total landed cost:** A USD 10,000 CIF electronics shipment to São Paulo can reach ~USD 17,000+ after all taxes (70% increase). ICMS in São Paulo is 18%.

**PRC (Remessa Conforme) Program:** For e-commerce parcels up to USD 50, the rate is reduced to a flat 20% import duty + 17% ICMS. Parcels USD 50.01–3,000: 60% (minus USD 20 discount) + ICMS. Non-PRC registered platforms: 60% with no discount. *This is the key regulatory framework for cross-border e-commerce imports.*

### 1.7 Special Economic Zones / Bonded Warehouses (Recintos Aduaneiros)

#### Types of Bonded Facilities in Brazil

| Type | Purpose | Key Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Entreposto Aduaneiro** (Bonded Warehouse) | Store imported goods under tax suspension until nationalization | Suspend II, IPI, ICMS, PIS, COFINS, AFRMM for up to 1 year (extendable); nationalize on-demand |
| **Porto Seco (Dry Port / EADI)** | Inland customs clearance stations | 31 registered; many in São Paulo and Rio Grande do Sul; enable customs clearance away from congested sea ports |
| **CLIA** (Container Logistics Installation) | Container handling + customs in secondary zone | Key ones: Santos, São Bernardo, Itajaí, Rio Grande, Manaus |
| **REDEX** | Export-dedicated customs station | 63 units; 30% located in Santos; for exporters |
| **Indústria Alfandegada** | Manufacturing inside bonded zone | Suspend taxes on imported inputs; final product can be exported or nationalized |

**Major EADI (Dry Ports) near São Paulo:**
- EADI Santo André (SP)
- EADI São Paulo (SP)
- EADI Curitiba (PR)
- EADI Manaus (AM) — Zona Franca de Manaus has additional benefits

**Bonded Warehouse Benefits:**
- Tax suspension (not exemption) up to 1 year
- Partial removal of goods allowed
- Goods can be transferred between bonded regimes
- Insurance covers losses/damages while in regime
- Compatible with Recof, Drawback, and other special regimes
- Dramatically reduces port storage (demurrage) costs at Santos

**Drawback Regime:** Key for manufacturers/trading companies — suspends or eliminates import taxes on inputs that will be used in export products. A trade facilitator acting as a trading company (trading) can leverage this.

**Zona Franca de Manaus (ZFM):** A special economic zone in Manaus, Amazon, with significant tax incentives including ICMS exemption and IPI reduction for manufacturing. Relevant if sourcing electronics assembled locally from Chinese components.

### 2.1 Cross-Border Payment Solutions (Paying Chinese Suppliers from Brazil)

#### The Dollar vs. Local Currency Landscape (2025)
Brazil and China have a **bilateral local currency settlement system** operational since 2023, managed through ICBC (chosen as Brazil-side clearing house by Chinese government). Key facts:
- A **BRL 157 billion (USD 27.7 billion) currency swap** was signed in May 2025 (5-year term)
- In Q1 2025, **41% of Brazil-China trade was settled in yuan** — a historic high
- Companies save **1–5% in financial costs** by eliminating double conversion via USD
- Any Brazilian company with a standard commercial bank account can access this — no special account in China required; simply request yuan settlement from your bank (Banco do Brasil, Itaú, Santander)

*Source: [BRICS Brasil](https://bricsbrasil.com.br/en/brazil-china-local-currency-trade/), [CPG Petróleo e Gás](https://clickpetroleoegas.com.br/brasil-firma-swap-cambial-de-r-157-bilhoes-com-a-china-e-fortalece-uso-do-yuan-pressao-sobre-a-hegemonia-do-dolar-vml97/)*

**Real-world example:** Chilli Beans (Brazilian eyewear, BRL 1.4B revenue) now pays all Chinese suppliers directly in yuan.

#### Payment Methods Available to Brazilian Importers

| Method | Provider | How It Works | Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **TT Wire Transfer (USD)** | Any commercial bank (Itaú, Bradesco, Santander, BB) | USD wire from Brazilian bank to Chinese supplier bank account | Bank spread ~1.5–3% + flat fee | Standard B2B payments |
| **TT Wire (Yuan/CNY)** | Banco do Brasil, Itaú, Santander | Direct real→yuan conversion via CIPS clearing | 1–5% savings vs. USD routing | Trade facilitators paying Chinese factories regularly |

| **CIPS (China's SWIFT alternative)** | Via Chinese and Brazilian banks | Direct interbank payment system | Similar to SWIFT cost | Large institutional transactions |

### 2.3 Trade Finance / Letters of Credit

#### Letter of Credit (L/C) — How It Works for China Imports
1. Brazilian importer applies to their Brazilian bank to issue L/C
2. Brazilian bank sends L/C to Chinese exporter's bank (via SWIFT or CIPS)
3. Chinese exporter ships goods and presents compliant documents
4. Brazilian bank verifies documents and pays — importer repays bank

**When to use L/C:** New supplier relationships, high-value shipments, situations where the supplier insists on L/C, when the importer needs time to pay (deferred/term credits: 30, 60, 90, 120 days after sight)

**Cost:** L/C issuance + amendment fees typically 1–3% of transaction value per year; minimum USD 200–500 flat.

**Key banks offering trade finance for Brazil-China route:**
- **Bank of China Brazil branch:** Issues import L/Cs; direct connection to Chinese banking system
- **ICBC Brazil:** Designated clearing house for BRL/CNY settlement; trade finance products
- **Banco do Brasil:** PROEX (export financing) and import financing; BNDES for larger operations
- **Itaú BBA:** Documentary credits, supply chain finance, receivables advance
- **Baker McKenzie note (Jan 2025):** Brazil has zero withholding tax for funds obtained abroad to finance exports (Drawback financing structure)

#### Other Trade Finance Instruments
- **Adiantamento sobre Contrato de Câmbio (ACC):** Pre-export financing for Brazilian exporters
- **Factoring / Receivables Finance:** Alternative financiers fund up to 100% of stock cost including freight
- **Open Account with Credit Insurance:** Sinosure (Chinese state-owned credit insurer) has been increasingly used by Brazilian importers of Chinese goods — relevant given April 2026 reports of Brazilian importers turning to Sinosure to sustain USD 158B trade flows *Source: [SCMP via Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/scmp/posts/brazilian-importers-are-turning-to-a-state-owned-chinese-credit-insurer-to-susta/1341543331355049/)*

### 2.4 Cargo Insurance

#### Coverage Structure

| Policy Type | What It Covers | Best For | Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| **All-Risk** | Most perils except exclusions | High-value electronics, machinery | 0.5–2% of cargo value |
| **Named Perils** | Specific listed risks (fire, sinking) | Low-value bulk cargo | <0.5% |

- South Atlantic storms (peak hurricane season)

- Container mishandling during long 35–45 day transit
- Congestion-related delays at Santos (7 free days before storage fees; always get warehouse-to-warehouse extension)

**2026 premium trend:** +10–15% expected due to climate risks and route congestion. *Source: [FreightAmigo](https://www.freightamigo.com/en/blog/transport-insurance/china-brazil-sea-freight-marine-insurance-2026-high-risk-route-protection/)*

**Key insurers:**
- **DHL Global Forwarding Brazil:** Arranges cargo insurance as part of freight service; full replacement value coverage *Source: [DHL Brazil](https://www.dhl.com/br-en/home/global-forwarding/products-and-solutions/cargo-insurance.html)*
- **Ping An Insurance (China):** Insures BL Shipping shipments; accessible through Chinese forwarders
- **Zurich, Allianz Brasil:** Standard cargo insurers in Brazil
- **B2B Freight platform:** Online cargo insurance for single or open (annual) coverage; single shipment vs. open policy; open policy most cost-efficient for regular importers

**Practical recommendation:** For a trade facilitator managing regular shipments, negotiate an **open cargo policy** (annual, covers all shipments) at ~0.5–0.8% of CIF value. Single-shipment policies cost more per shipment.

### 3.1 Mercado Livre — Mercado Envios Full

Mercado Livre is the **#1 marketplace in Latin America** with 242 million monthly visits in Brazil and is the largest warehouse occupier in the country.

#### How Mercado Envios Full Works
1. Seller sends inventory to Mercado Livre's distribution center (CD)
2. ML receives, registers, organizes stock in their system
3. When sold: ML picks, packs, labels, and ships
4. Post-sale (returns, customer service): ML handles

**Benefits of Full:**
- Products appear with "Full" badge → **up to 3× more sales** (ML's own data; 75% of buyers prefer Full products)
- Next-day and same-day delivery (even Saturdays)
- Algorithm boost: Full products rank higher in search
- Freight discounts up to 50% for sellers using Full vs. self-fulfillment
- MercadoLíder sellers get partial freight reimbursement for products R$99+

#### Requirements to Access Full
Full is **by invitation** (ML sends when seller qualifies) but sellers can accelerate eligibility by:
- Maintaining green reputation (minimum)
- Achieving **15–30 sales/month for 3–6 months minimum**
- Filling out the Full interest form: `https://envios.mercadolibre.com.mx/vender-con-full/contacto`
- Once approved: access to "Gestão de Envios Full" dashboard

**Product size/weight limits:**
- Max weight: 25 kg per package
- Max single dimension: 120 cm
- Sum of 3 dimensions: ≤ 260 cm

#### Costs of Mercado Envios Full (Brazil, 2026 rates — updated March 2, 2026)

| Fee | Amount | Trigger |

**Selling on Mercado Livre — Basic Requirements:**
- Brazilian CPF (individuals) or CNPJ (businesses — recommended for volume)

- Tax regime: Simples Nacional (with Inscrição Estadual in SP, PR, RJ, MG, SC, RS, CE, DF, or PE) OR Regime Normal (IE in SP)
- Products must comply with Amazon packaging/safety requirements

#### How a Trade Facilitator Can Leverage Amazon FBA
- Import in bulk → store in Amazon's CD → sellers list with FBA
- Amazon handles Prime delivery to consumers across Brazil
- Combine with FBA Onsite if maintaining a local warehouse
- Amazon's same-day delivery now covers 7+ major cities (as of Oct 2025)

### 3.3 Shopee Brazil — Logistics and Fulfillment Model

Shopee Brazil has emerged as a formidable #2 behind Mercado Livre:
- **GMV: BRL 70 billion in 2025** (up from ~50B in 2024; ~30% annual growth)
- **15 major distribution centers** across Brazil (as of Jan 2026)
- **180+ regional logistics hubs**
- **3,000+ partner collection points**
- Now the **2nd-largest warehouse occupier** in Brazil (surpassing Amazon); only behind Mercado Livre

**Shopee Express:** Shopee's proprietary logistics network (SLS - Shopee Logistics Service). Shopee handles first/middle mile in many areas; last mile via partners (Correios, Total Express, local carriers).

**Free shipping threshold:** Reduced to **R$19** — aggressively low to drive user acquisition.

**Seller Fulfillment Options on Shopee Brazil:**
1. **Self-Fulfillment (FBM - Fulfilled by Merchant):** Seller packs and ships; Shopee provides label and carrier access
2. **Shopee Logistics (SLS):** Shopee manages first/middle/last mile
3. **Shopee Fulfillment (where available):** Stock in Shopee's DCs; similar to FBA but Shopee-managed

#### Requirements to Sell on Shopee Brazil
- Brazilian CPF or CNPJ
- Active bank account
- Product compliance with Shopee's category policies
- No initial volume requirement (open marketplace)
- Shopee's commission structure: category-dependent (typically 12–18%)
- Shopee Moedas (loyalty currency) and promotional mechanics drive discovery

**Strategic Note for Trade Facilitators:** Shopee's rapid expansion of its own DC network (15 DCs + 180+ hubs) means it increasingly controls the fulfillment chain. A trade facilitator with inventory already in Brazil can offer Shopee sellers same-day/next-day fulfillment via Shopee's DCs, significantly improving conversion. Shopee is particularly strong with price-sensitive consumers and Chinese-origin goods already have brand recognition.

### 3.4 How a Trade Facilitator Can Leverage Marketplace DCs

**The core model:**
```
China factory → Consolidation warehouse (Shenzhen/Yiwu) → FCL ocean freight → 
Santos port → Bonded warehouse (tax suspension) → Nationalize on demand → 
Split inventory across: ML Full DC + Amazon FBA DC + Shopee DC → 
Each marketplace handles last-mile delivery to consumers
```

**Benefits:**
- No need to build own last-mile delivery network
- ML/Amazon/Shopee absorb returns/customer service
- Full badge on ML, Prime on Amazon = premium positioning and 2–3× conversion
- Marketplace DCs often better located for fast delivery than any independent warehouse
- Inventory visibility across all channels via ERP integrations

**Revenue model for trade facilitator:**
- Markup on goods (import price + taxes + fulfillment margin)
- Fulfillment service fee to client brands using facilitator's import license/CNPJ
- Volume rebates from shipping carriers (negotiated on combined volume)
- Advisory/compliance fees for navigating RADAR, INMETRO, ANVISA approvals

Quality inspection (controle de qualidade / inspeção de qualidade) is critical before shipment. Major providers have inspectors based in all Chinese manufacturing hubs.

|---|---|---|---|---|

| **Intertek** | Global TIC | London | Consumer goods, electronics, food safety; INMETRO certification support | Similar to SGS |

| **CCIC (China Certification)** | State-owned | Beijing | Commodities; coal, minerals; also general goods | Government-backed |

#### Standard Inspection Types and Costs

| Inspection Type | Timing | Typical Cost | What It Catches |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-Production Inspection (PPI)** | Before manufacturing starts | USD 200–350 | Material quality, supplier readiness |
| **During Production Inspection (DUPRO)** | 30–50% through production | USD 250–450 | Defects early enough to correct |
| **Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI)** | After 80–100% production complete | USD 250–450 (1 day) | Final AQL sampling before shipping |
| **Container Loading Supervision (CLS)** | At loading | USD 200–350 (4–8 hrs) | Correct goods loaded, quantities verified |
| **Factory Audit** | Supplier qualification | USD 600–1,200 (1–2 days) | Manufacturing capability, labor practices |
| **Lab Testing** | Material/safety compliance | USD 100–800+ per test | Regulatory compliance (CE, INMETRO, ANVISA) |

**Industry note:** Most standard random inspections for consumer goods are completed in **1 man-day** using AQL (Acceptable Quality Limit) Level II sampling. A man-day typically includes travel within 60km of inspector's base. Extra travel: +USD 50 (120km), +USD 100-150 (400km).

**Alibaba integration:** When ordering via Alibaba, platform offers inspection add-on by SGS, CCIC, or Bureau Veritas for approximately **USD 188 flat** — basic but convenient for first orders.

#### Brazil-Specific Compliance Certifications (Often Require China Testing)
- **INMETRO:** Mandatory for electronics, electrical products, toys, PPE; can be tested in China by accredited labs (TÜV, SGS, Intertek) then certified in Brazil
- **ANVISA:** Mandatory for cosmetics, food supplements, medical devices, pharmaceuticals; Brazilian registration required before import
- **ANATEL:** Mandatory for telecommunications equipment; RF testing required
- **MAPA:** For agricultural inputs, animal products, plant-based products

**For a trade facilitator:** Building relationships with 2–3 inspection companies (e.g., SGS/QIMA for premium clients, V-Trust/TESTCOO for budget) allows offering tiered QC packages. Standard PSI is typically a pass-through cost (USD 250–350) billed to the importing client.
