## Category 1: Sourcing Agents & Platforms

Companies that help Brazilian businesses source goods directly from Chinese factories.

### China Gate Importação
- **Founded:** 2013 | **HQ:** Maringá, Paraná | **Size:** 11–50 employees
- **Business Model:** End-to-end import facilitation for Brazilian SMEs. Fixed price per cubic meter (preço fixo por m³). Provides LCL (shared container) consolidation with weekly departures from China. Also sells courses and educational content for importers.
- **Services:** Supplier sourcing, cargo consolidation, customs clearance, Radar Siscomex habilitation, import consulting, Inmetro certification assistance, air and sea freight.
- **Target Customer:** Brazilian entrepreneurs and SMEs importing from China for the first time or scaling; MEI to medium companies.
- **Pricing Model:** Fixed per-m³ freight rate (LCL); service fees for consulting. Quoted as ~R$2.20/unit vs. competitor ~R$1.65 (Reddit user anecdote, likely per kg).
- **Key Differentiators:** Strong brand among first-time importers; active content marketing on Instagram/YouTube; partnership with Rakumart for logistics/customs.
- **Geographic Presence:** Brazil (Maringá HQ) + partner offices in China.
- **Website:** chinagate.com.br

### China Link Trading
- **Founded:** 2009 | **HQ:** Santos, SP (Matriz)
- **Business Model:** "360° ecosystem" positioning itself as an import accelerator rather than just a trading company. Full-service from supplier audit to delivery. Organizes trade fair trips to China (Canton Fair, etc.).
- **Services:** Supplier sourcing, factory audit, quality inspection, logistics coordination, customs clearance, trade fair organization (China fairs), Radar Siscomex habilitação, cost simulation.
- **Target Customer:** Mid-sized Brazilian companies seeking to scale China imports; entrepreneurs who want to systematize their sourcing.
- **Pricing Model:** Project-based consulting fees. Not publicly listed.
- **Key Differentiators:** 6 offices (Santos, Curitiba, Itajaí in Brazil; Shenzhen, Ningbo, Hong Kong in China); claims to be market leader; community/networking events; "Método Link" program.
- **Geographic Presence:** Brazil + China (Shenzhen, Ningbo, Hong Kong).
- **Website:** chinalinktrading.com.br

### JoomPro (Joom Group)
- **Founded Brazil entry:** 2023 | **HQ:** São Paulo (Av. Paulista) + Florianópolis fiscal address
- **Parent:** Joom Group (Portuguese holding, HQ Lisbon/Luxembourg)
- **Business Model:** B2B import management platform. Functions as an import manager rather than a marketplace — clients browse 18M+ products from 130K Chinese suppliers across 3,700 categories, submit a cart/RFQ, and JoomPro handles everything end-to-end.
- **Services:** Product catalog with estimated landed costs, quotation, sampling (physical or virtual), product customization/OEM, quality control in China, international freight, Brazilian customs clearance, domestic delivery to client's warehouse. Also offers import financing and Radar Siscomex support.
- **Target Customer:** Brazilian businesses (any size, including MEI) that want to import Chinese products without managing individual vendors, forwarders, or customs agents.
- **Pricing Model:** Product prices shown in BRL with embedded margins; specific service fees not published. Announced investment of up to US$50M over 5 years in Brazil.
- **Key Differentiators:** Visual product search (image search); financing for imports; single platform covering all 8 steps of import process; ~40 employees in Brazil + ~40 in China (as of 2024).
- **Website:** joom.pro/pt-br

### Rakumart
- **Origin:** Chinese company (Zhejiang Hangzhou, linked to 1688.com ecosystem)
- **Business Model:** B2B marketplace/aggregator that mirrors products from 1688 (China's domestic wholesale platform, cheaper than Alibaba) and translates them into Portuguese. Brazilian importers browse, select, and Rakumart handles consolidation, quality control, freight, and customs. Acts as an intermediary/trading agent.
- **Services:** Product search (including image search), supplier vetting, consolidation warehouse in China, quality inspection, international freight (sea LCL or air), Brazilian customs clearance, door-to-door delivery.
- **Target Customer:** Small Brazilian businesses/entrepreneurs with CNPJ who want to import small quantities legally without speaking Mandarin. Key use case: importing from 1688 (not accessible directly in Portuguese).
- **Pricing Model:** Commission-based. Adds margin to 1688 factory prices; all-in quotes include freight and duties. Cheaper than Alibaba for equivalent products due to 1688 access.
- **Key Differentiators:** Access to 1688 (Chinese domestic prices, cheaper than Alibaba); Portuguese-language platform; WhatsApp-based agent support; partnership with China Gate for customs/freight; Google Chrome extension for price comparison.
- **Website:** rakumart.com.br

## Category 2: Cross-Border E-Commerce Platforms

Chinese consumer platforms operating B2C in Brazil.

### AliExpress (Alibaba Group)
- **Business Model:** Marketplace connecting Chinese sellers directly to Brazilian consumers. Operates under Brazil's **Remessa Conforme** (Programa de Conformidade) program since 2023, which reduced import duty from 60% to 20% for orders under US$50 for participating platforms in exchange for tax registration and upfront payment of duties.
- **Fulfillment Model:** Sellers ship from China or from AliExpress local warehouses. Cainiao (Alibaba's logistics arm) handles cross-border logistics. Delivery times dropped from 40 days (2021) to as low as 4 days (2024) in São Paulo.
- **Target Customer:** Individual consumers (B2C) and small businesses.
- **Key Differentiators:** Massive product selection; Cainiao logistics infrastructure in Brazil; AliExpress Choice program for curated fast delivery; strong brand recognition.
- **Tax/Regulatory:** Fully compliant with Remessa Conforme; 20% import duty on items ≤ US$50.
- **Recent Developments:** Increasingly pushing sellers to list under Remessa Conforme; sellers outside the program face growing restrictions on Brazil-bound shipments.

---

### Shopee (Sea Group, Singapore)
- **Business Model:** Marketplace with a predominantly local seller base (90%+ of sales from Brazilian sellers as of 2025). Entering a dual model — domestic marketplace + some cross-border Chinese goods.
- **Fulfillment Model:** 19 distribution centers in Brazil (14 cross-docking + 5 fulfillment as of April 2026); 200+ logistics hubs; 20K+ driver partners; 400+ "Agências Shopee" pickup points. Fulfillment model (SPX/Shopee handles storage + packing + shipping): launched October 2024, 30% improvement in delivery time. 5th fulfillment center opened in MG (April 2026).
- **Target Customer:** Brazilian consumers (broad demographic, high mobile penetration).
- **Key Differentiators:** Best-in-class logistics network in Brazil for a marketplace; local seller dominance; deep market penetration; SPX Shopee Express (own carrier).
- **Tax/Regulatory:** Registered in Remessa Conforme for Chinese goods.

---

### Shein
- **Business Model:** Originally: fast fashion direct-from-China DTC. In Brazil: hybrid model — shifting aggressively toward local production and a Brazilian marketplace.
- **Fulfillment Model:** 3 distribution centers in São Paulo. Local sourcing: as of February 2025, **75% of products** on the Brazilian platform are locally made; **85% of women's/men's apparel and footwear** are produced in Brazil (300+ partner Brazilian factories). Marketplace: 30,000+ Brazilian sellers; 50 million Brazilian customers.
- **Target Customer:** Brazilian consumers (strong focus on women's fashion, Gen Z/millennial); also Brazilian sellers via the marketplace.
- **Pricing Model:** DTC; platform commission for marketplace sellers.
- **Key Differentiators:** Fastest pivot to local production of any Chinese platform; 50M customer base in Brazil; 45M+ loyal Brazilian users. Joined Remessa Conforme.
- **Tax/Regulatory:** Joined Remessa Conforme (2023); publicly opposed the "taxa das blusinhas" (20% import duty on ≤US$50 items). Local production makes the import tax less relevant for Shein than for pure-cross-border players.
- **Recent:** Profit dropped ~40% globally in 2024; expanding into SC textile cluster (Blumenau, Joinville, Brusque, Itajaí).

---

### Temu (PDD Holdings / Pinduoduo)
- **Business Model:** Ultra-low-price consumer marketplace, originally China-direct. In Brazil: adding a "local-to-local" seller program.
- **Fulfillment Model:** Launched in Brazil June 6, 2024. Initially pure cross-border (ship from China). Opened seller enrollment to Brazilian sellers (local-to-local model active in 30+ markets including Brazil). Registered with Remessa Conforme (certified May 15, 2024).
- **Target Customer:** Price-sensitive Brazilian consumers.
- **Key Differentiators:** Extreme low prices (90% discount promotions at launch); gamified shopping experience; aggressive marketing spend.
- **Tax/Regulatory:** Remessa Conforme registered; 20% import duty on ≤US$50 items.
- **Recent:** Rapidly expanding product categories; latest 2025 initiative: local sellers can now sell on Temu for 2-day delivery.

---

### Mercado Livre (MercadoLibre, MELI)
- **Business Model:** LatAm's dominant e-commerce marketplace. Increasingly adding China cross-border capability as a competitive response.
- **Fulfillment Model:** Opened first **China fulfillment hub** (December 2025) — "Fulfilled from China" model. Products ship from Chinese factories through Mercado Livre's China hub to Brazil/Colombia/Chile/Mexico/Argentina in ~30 days. Cross-border grew 74% in Q4 2025. Domestic: massive Meli logistics network (14 domestic fulfillment centers planned in 2026 in Brazil).
- **Target Customer:** Full range of Brazilian consumers and sellers.
- **Key Differentiators:** Dominant market position in Brazil (52% of MELI revenue); Meli Pago payments; full logistics control (Meli Envios); entering China supply chain directly to compete with Asian platforms on price.
- **Note:** Not a China-sourcing facilitator per se, but strategically relevant as a marketplace that increasingly routes Chinese inventory directly to Brazilian consumers.

## Category 3: Trading Companies & Brazilian Importadoras

Full-service import companies handling all aspects of the import process for Brazilian businesses.

### Overview of the Brazilian "Trading Company" Model
Brazilian trading companies operate under three main legal modalities:
1. **Importação por Conta Própria** – The trading imports on its own account and resells to Brazilian companies.
2. **Importação por Conta e Ordem** – The trading imports on behalf of a Brazilian client (client funds; trading handles all bureaucracy).
3. **Importação por Encomenda** – Trading imports and re-sells to a specific ordering company.

Fiscal incentives in Santa Catarina (Itajaí, Navegantes ports) via TTD (Tratamento Tributário Diferenciado) are commonly leveraged for ICMS reduction, making SC ports a hub for trading company operations.

## Category 5: Freight Forwarders & Logistics Companies

### Cainiao (Alibaba Group)
- **Brazil entry:** 2021 (Latin America HQ opened São Paulo, November 2022)
- **Business Model:** Cross-border and domestic logistics network. Primary logistics arm for AliExpress in Brazil. Also serves Brazilian merchants exporting to China.
- **Brazil Infrastructure:** 4 international distribution centers (covering all cities via Correios partnership); operates 8 chartered cargo flights/week between China and Brazil. Signed strategic cooperation agreement with Brazil's Correios (2023). Delivery time improvement: from 40 days (2021) → 15 days → 4 days (2024) in São Paulo.
- **Key Differentiators:** AI-driven logistics (demand prediction, route optimization, RFID, sorters, good-to-person robotics); signed bilateral strategic agreement with Correios (first logistics inclusion in a Brazil-China bilateral document); 12–19% reduction in logistics costs via tech improvements.
- **Model:** "Local management" model in Brazil (rare for Cainiao — most markets managed from China).
- **Plans:** 9+ more distribution centers across 7 Brazilian states; 1,000 smart lockers in 10 cities; Brazil-China air and sea routes.

- **Founded:** 2015 (Indonesia) | **Brazil entry:** February 2022

### Strategic Observations

**1. The "Remessa Conforme" tax regime is the key regulatory lever**
All major cross-border platforms operate under this program. The government's ability to adjust the US$50 threshold (and the 20% import duty) is the single biggest regulatory risk for the China-Brazil cross-border sector. Every platform is now compliant, but the looming IVA (VAT reform, starting 2026) will affect all platforms equally.

**2. Logistics is the key moat**
Cainiao's investment in Brazil (dropping delivery times from 40 days → 4 days) and Shopee's 19-DC network represent the largest competitive advantages for Chinese players. J&T Express provides the carrier rails. Brazilian domestic logistics is the biggest cost driver and differentiator.

**3. B2B sourcing is highly fragmented with no dominant tech player**
Unlike the B2C market (which has clear leaders), the B2B sourcing facilitation market is fragmented among dozens of trading companies, agents, and platforms. JoomPro and Rakumart are the most tech-forward, but both still rely heavily on human agents. There is no true "Alibaba.com for Brazil" — a self-serve, digitally-native, end-to-end import platform with significant scale.

**4. Localization is the competitive response to tariffs**
Shein's pivot to local production in Brazil (75% local as of early 2025) demonstrates that Chinese platforms are willing to fundamentally restructure their supply chains to maintain Brazilian market access. This reduces the competitive moat of "we help you import from China" for companies targeting fashion/apparel categories.

**5. Mercado Livre entering China supply chain is a game-changer**
Opening a China fulfillment hub in December 2025 means Brazil's dominant marketplace is now sourcing and fulfilling directly from Chinese factories. This threatens both Chinese platforms (competing on Mercado Livre's home turf) and Brazilian import facilitators who serve Mercado Livre sellers.

**6. Brazilian trade via Santa Catarina ports is the fiscal optimization hub**
ICMS reduction via SC's TTD regime (Itajaí, Navegantes, São Francisco do Sul, Itapoá ports) is a de facto competitive advantage that many trading companies and importadoras leverage. Companies registered in SC for import purposes can significantly reduce landed costs.
