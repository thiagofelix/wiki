# China–Brazil Bilateral Trade Landscape: Comprehensive Research Report

**Research period covered:** 2023–2025 (with select historical context)  
**Last updated:** July 2025

---

## Executive Summary

China has been Brazil's largest trading partner for 17 consecutive years. In 2025, bilateral trade hit a record **US$171 billion**, more than double Brazil's total trade with the United States ([South China Morning Post](https://www.scmp.com/news/china/diplomacy/article/3339924/brazil-china-trade-hits-record-us171-billion-2025-us-tariffs-prompt-export-pivot)). The relationship is structurally asymmetric: Brazil exports commodities (soybeans, iron ore, crude oil) while China exports high-value manufactured goods. Cross-border e-commerce has emerged as a fast-growing second channel, led by Chinese platforms—Shopee, Shein, AliExpress, and Temu—that have collectively penetrated Brazil's digital retail economy, generating regulatory tension and triggering a significant overhaul of Brazil's import taxation regime through the Remessa Conforme program.

## 1. Total Bilateral Trade Volume (2023–2025) and Growth Trends

### Annual Trade Volumes

| Year | Total Trade (USD) | Brazil Exports to China | Brazil Imports from China | Trade Surplus for Brazil |
|------|-------------------|------------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| 2023 | ~US$157.5–181.5B* | ~US$100–122.4B | ~US$56–59.1B | ~US$48.3–63.3B |
| 2024 | ~US$158B | ~US$116B | ~US$72B | ~US$44B |
| 2025 | US$171B (record) | US$100B | US$70.9B | US$29.1B |

*Note: Figures differ across sources due to measurement methodology (Brazilian MDIC vs. Chinese GACC customs statistics, CIF vs. FOB). Chinese customs data typically yields a larger bilateral total. The most commonly cited figures from the China-Brazil Business Council (CEBC) show US$171 billion in 2025.*

**Key data points:**

- In 2023, the Brazilian Ministry of Development and Industry (MDIC) recorded **US$157.5 billion** in total bilateral trade, the highest ever in its historical series at the time, with Brazilian exports to China crossing US$100 billion for the first time ([IEDI Letter No. 1292](https://www.iedi.org.br/artigos/textos/english/letter_iedi_n_1292.html)).
- Chinese customs statistics for 2023 showed a slightly higher figure of **US$181.53 billion**, with China's exports to Brazil at US$59.11 billion (down 4.3% year-on-year) and imports from Brazil at US$122.42 billion (up 11.9%) ([China Briefing](https://www.china-briefing.com/news/china-brazil-economic-ties-trade-investment-and-opportunities/)).
- In 2024, bilateral trade reached approximately **US$158 billion** (CEBC/MDIC) or **US$188.17 billion** (Chinese GACC), with Chinese exports to Brazil surging 23.3% to RMB 512.82 billion (USD 72.08 billion) while Brazilian exports to China dipped 4.4% ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)).
- In Jan–Oct 2024, Chinese customs data showed trade growth of **9.9%** year-on-year, 4.7 percentage points higher than China's overall foreign trade growth rate ([China's State Council](https://english.www.gov.cn/archive/statistics/202411/17/content_WS6739ebacc6d0868f4e8ed161.html)).
- In 2025, the CEBC reported trade reached **US$171 billion**, an 8.2% increase year-on-year, with Brazil's imports from China climbing 11.5% to a record **US$70.9 billion**, driven by an oil exploration platform, electric and hybrid vehicles, fertilizers, and pharmaceutical inputs ([Global Times](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202601/1353165.shtml), [The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/aseanplus/aseanplus-news/2026/01/15/brazil-china-trade-hits-record-us171bil-in-2025-as-us-tariffs-prompt-export-pivot)).
- China accounted for **27.2% of Brazil's total foreign trade** in 2025, versus the US (second partner) at roughly half that share ([Global Times](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202601/1353165.shtml)).

### Growth Trajectory

The long-run growth is stark. Bilateral trade grew from **US$3.2 billion in 2001 to US$158 billion in 2024—a nearly 50-fold increase** over 23 years ([CEIC Data](https://info.ceicdata.com/ceic-article-how-chinese-trade-reshaped-brazil)). China surpassed the US as Brazil's number one trade partner around 2009–2010 and has widened that gap every year since. Between 2019 and 2023, Brazilian exports to China rose 64.7% while Chinese exports to Brazil rose 47.6% ([IEDI](https://www.iedi.org.br/artigos/textos/english/letter_iedi_n_1292.html)).

### Brazil's Trade Surplus

Brazil has maintained a trade surplus with China for 17 consecutive years. The surplus peaked at approximately **US$63 billion in 2023** (per Chinese customs data), narrowed to around **US$44 billion in 2024** as Chinese exports surged, and further narrowed to **US$29.1 billion in 2025**—still accounting for 43% of Brazil's entire US$68.3 billion global trade surplus that year ([The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/aseanplus/aseanplus-news/2026/01/15/brazil-china-trade-hits-record-us171bil-in-2025-as-us-tariffs-prompt-export-pivot)).

### Notable Emerging Categories

**Electric Vehicles (EVs) and New Energy Vehicles (NEVs):** The fastest-growing import category. In the first four months of 2024, China's passenger car exports to Brazil reached **US$762 million**, a 372.4% year-on-year increase. Chinese EV sales in Brazil reached 48,000 units in just the first four months of 2024—eight times the same period in the previous year—capturing 36.2% of Brazil's total imported EV volume ([China Briefing](https://www.china-briefing.com/news/china-brazil-economic-ties-trade-investment-and-opportunities/)). In 2024 overall, **more than 80% of the 61,615 EVs sold in Brazil were Chinese brands**, primarily BYD and GWM ([AP News](https://apnews.com/article/china-latin-trump-trade-jinping-3bd62dd2d73ed711fa754ead1997634a)). In H1 2024, Chinese NEVs accounted for **91% of all NEVs imported into Brazil** ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)).

**Pharmaceuticals:** Medicines and pharmaceutical inputs rose sharply in 2025, making China the **fourth-largest supplier to Brazil** in this segment ([Global Times](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202601/1353165.shtml)).

**Solar Panels:** Imports of solar panels from China rose 13% in Jan–Mar 2025, setting a quarterly record ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)).

**Fertilizers:** Imports of phosphorus- and nitrogen-based fertilizers surged **625% to 265,000 tons** in a single quarter in 2025 ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)).

**Intermediate Goods Composition:** In Jan–Oct 2024, China exported intermediate goods worth 216.86 billion yuan to Brazil, up 11.8% year-on-year—accounting for **half of China's total export value to Brazil** ([China State Council](https://english.www.gov.cn/archive/statistics/202411/17/content_WS6739ebacc6d0868f4e8ed161.html)). This underscores that much of what Brazil imports from China feeds directly into domestic industrial production, not just consumer markets.

China also supplied **27% of Brazil's manufactured goods** imports in 2025, "well ahead of the United States and Germany" ([The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/aseanplus/aseanplus-news/2026/01/15/brazil-china-trade-hits-record-us171bil-in-2025-as-us-tariffs-prompt-export-pivot)).

## 3. Key Trade Routes and Logistics Corridors

### Primary Maritime Routes

The overwhelming majority of China–Brazil trade moves by sea. Three main routing axes exist:

**1. Trans-Atlantic via the Cape of Good Hope / Around Africa**  
The dominant deep-sea route for containerized cargo from Chinese ports (Shanghai, Ningbo, Qingdao, Shenzhen, Xiamen) to Brazil's main ports. Transit times typically range from 35 to 48 days, depending on the port pair and carrier. Major carriers include CMA CGM, MSC, Maersk, ONE, and Yang Ming.

**2. Trans-Pacific via the Panama Canal**  
For many commodity and agricultural flows, especially soybeans (exported from Brazil to China), the Panama Canal route is dominant. Approximately **90% of China–Brazil soybean shipments pass through the Panama Canal**, creating strategic dependency on this choke point and vulnerability to US influence over the waterway ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)).

**3. New Direct Santana–Zhuhai Route (inaugurated August 2025)**  
Brazil formally inaugurated a new direct maritime route in August 2025 linking the **Port of Santana (Amapá state, northern Brazil) to the Port of Gaolan in Zhuhai, China**. The route, which also connects to Salvador (Bahia), reduces transportation times by up to **30 days** and cuts logistics costs by more than **30%** compared to prior routing ([Global Times](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202508/1342198.shtml), [Fresh Fruit Portal](https://www.freshfruitportal.com/news/2025/04/21/new-direct-maritime-route-between-brazil-and-china-to-boost-brazilian-fruit-exports/)). This route is integral to expanding exports of fresh produce (grapes, melons) from the Brazilian Northeast to China.

### Key Brazilian Ports

**Port of Santos (São Paulo state)** is by far the most critical node in Brazil–China trade. In 2025, it set an all-time record by handling **186.4 million tonnes** of cargo, up 3.6% from the prior record of 179.8 million tonnes in 2024. China was the port's main trading partner, accounting for **29.6% of total cargo flows** ([DatamarNews](https://datamarnews.com/noticias/brazils-port-of-santos-posts-record-cargo-volumes-in-2025/)). Santos handles approximately 40% of Brazil's soybean exports destined for China. The port's grain export capacity is already at **91% utilization**.

Chinese state-owned agribusiness company **COFCO is investing US$285 million** in a new grain export terminal at Santos that will triple its shipping capacity from 4.5 million to up to 14 million tonnes per year, with direct access to Rumo's railway network ([YouTube / CPG Click Oil and Gas](https://en.clickpetroleoegas.com.br/china-e-brasil-montam-corredor-de-185-bilhoes-que-liga-ferrovias-ao-porto-de-chancay-encurta-caminho-entre-atlantico-e-pacifico-btl96/)).

**Other key ports:** Port of Paranaguá (Paraná), Port of Itaqui (Maranhão), and Port of Vitória (Espírito Santo) all serve as secondary export/import hubs. Vitória handles regional manufacturing and import flows from Chinese ports via transshipment through Santos or Miami.

### Emerging Corridor: Bioceanic Rail–Chancay Route

The most geopolitically significant emerging logistics corridor is the proposed **Bioceanic Corridor**—a multimodal rail route connecting Brazil's Atlantic agricultural heartland to the **Port of Chancay, Peru** (on the Pacific), which was inaugurated in November 2024 with **$3.4 billion in investment from COSCO** (which owns 60%) ([Mongabay](https://news.mongabay.com/short-article/brazil-china-move-ahead-on-3000-km-railway-crossing-the-amazon/)).

In July 2025, Brazilian state enterprise Infra S.A. and China Railway Economic and Planning Research Institute signed a technical cooperation agreement to study a ~3,000 km railway linking Mato Grosso (Brazil's soybean heartland) through Rondônia and Acre to Chancay, with an estimated investment of **US$3.5 billion** ([CPG Click Oil and Gas](https://en.clickpetroleoegas.com.br/china-e-brasil-montam-corredor-de-185-bilhoes-que-liga-ferrovias-ao-porto-de-chancay-encurta-caminho-entre-atlantico-e-pacifico-btl96/)). The backbone railways involved are FIOL (West-East Integration Railway, Bahia–Goiás), FICO, and Norte Sul. The corridor is conceived as a "land Panama Canal" that would allow agricultural commodities to reach China via the Pacific, bypassing the Panama Canal entirely. **Peru has not yet authorized investment in this project** ([Fundación Andrés Bello](https://www.fundacionandresbello.org/en/news/peru-🇵🇪-news/peru-clarifies-its-position-on-the-bioceanic-railway-project-proposed-by-brazil-and-china/)), and the project remains in the study phase.

## 4. The Evolution of Cross-Border E-Commerce Between China and Brazil

### Pre-2023 Context: The "Gray Zone" Era

Before August 2023, cross-border e-commerce from China to Brazil operated in a murky regulatory environment. Brazil's imported packages via postal services surged **338.52%** from 52.8 million in 2020 to 178.7 million in 2022, yet only 3.17% of these had registered customs declarations. Estimated uncollected ICMS and import duties between 2020 and 2022 exceeded **US$4.2 billion (R$21.1 billion)** ([WCO News](https://mag.wcoomd.org/magazine/wco-news-103/strengthening-e-commerce-compliance-overview-of-brazil-programa-remessa-conforme/)). Most platforms exploited the exemption for personal "gifts" under US$50, effectively enabling duty-free cross-border B2C sales at scale.

### 2023: The Remessa Conforme Transition

The Remessa Conforme program launched in August 2023 (detailed in Section 8) began to formalize the sector. International parcels under the program started arriving in Brazil by the end of September 2023. Tax compliance surged—tax collection from the program in Aug–Dec 2023 was **222.91% higher** than the corresponding period in 2022, yielding an additional **R$386 million (US$77 million)** ([WCO News](https://mag.wcoomd.org/magazine/wco-news-103/strengthening-e-commerce-compliance-overview-of-brazil-programa-remessa-conforme/)).

### 2024: Tax Tightening and Platform Adaptation

In August 2024, the exemption from import duty was eliminated even for PRC-enrolled platforms. Brazilian consumers spent **R$15 billion on approximately 190 million international orders in 2024**, a new record. This compares to R$6.42 billion in 2023 ([Reddit/G1 Globo report](https://www.reddit.com/r/AliExpressBR/comments/1lgbn8i/brasileiros_gastaram_quase_r_15_bilh%C3%B5es_em/)). Government tax collection from international parcels reached **R$2.88 billion in 2024**, a 45% increase over R$1.98 billion in 2023—yet platforms' revenues grew 140%, showing the regulatory change did not stem demand.

Despite the new 20% import tax on sub-$50 parcels, the volume of cross-border purchases continued to set records. BTG Pactual's analysts noted that "the 2024 regulatory change caused a sharp drop in the volume of cross-border orders, but did not eliminate [Chinese platforms'] competitive advantage" ([NeoFeed](https://neofeed.com.br/negocios/nem-a-taxa-das-blusinhas-barrou-shopee-shein-temu-e-tiktok-shop-avancam-no-brasil/en/)).

### 2025: Maturation and New Players

Brazil's cross-border e-commerce logistics market generated **US$1.637 billion in 2024** and is forecast to reach **US$6.17 billion by 2030**, implying a CAGR of 25.3% ([Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/horizon/outlook/cross-border-e-commerce-logistics-market/brazil)). Cross-border e-commerce was expected to constitute **7% of all online sales in Brazil in 2025** ([US ITA](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-ecommerce)).

New entrants in 2024–2025 include **Temu** (entered late 2024) and **TikTok Shop** (launched May 2025, reaching US$1 million/day in GMV within months of launch) ([NeoFeed](https://neofeed.com.br/negocios/nem-a-taxa-das-blusinhas-barrou-shopee-shein-temu-e-tiktok-shop-avancam-no-brasil/en/)).

Brazil's overall B2C e-commerce market reached **US$58.31 billion in 2024** (CAGR of 17.8% during 2020–2024) and is expected to hit **US$64.09 billion in 2025**, growing to US$89.18 billion by 2029 ([BusinessWire/ResearchAndMarkets](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260129960153/en/Brazil-B2C-Ecommerce-Market-Report-2025-2029)).

## 5. Chinese E-Commerce Platforms in Brazil: AliExpress, Shopee, Shein, Temu

As of January 2025, **four of the ten largest e-commerce platforms in Brazil by traffic are Chinese**: Shopee, Temu, Shein, and AliExpress. Together with Samsung (South Korean), these five recorded **639 million accesses** in January 2025 alone ([Forum Macao](https://www.forumchinaplp.org.mo/en/economic_trade/view/8701)).

### Shopee (Sea Group, Singapore-founded, Chinese operational DNA)

Shopee is the second-largest e-commerce platform in Brazil by GMV and the largest among the Chinese-heritage platforms. Key metrics:

- **2024 GMV**: approximately **BRL 50 billion** (US$~8.5 billion), projected to reach **BRL 70 billion in 2025** (+30% year-on-year) ([Shopdora/BTG Pactual](https://blog.shopdora.com/en/page/up-30-yoy-shopees-breakout-year-in-brazil-is-just-beginning/))
- **Monthly active users**: over **50 million** in Brazil as of early 2025 ([Rio Times](https://www.riotimesonline.com/temu-surpasses-mercado-livre-as-brazils-second-largest-marketplace/))
- **Market share** (including Asian platforms): approximately **14%** of Brazil's total e-commerce GMV ([NeoFeed](https://neofeed.com.br/negocios/nem-a-taxa-das-blusinhas-barrou-shopee-shein-temu-e-tiktok-shop-avancam-no-brasil/en/))
- **Logistics**: Shopee is the **second-largest warehouse tenant in Brazil**, operating more than 13 major distribution centers plus hundreds of sorting hubs; ~90% of Shopee Brasil's GMV comes from local sellers ([Shopdora](https://blog.shopdora.com/en/page/up-30-yoy-shopees-breakout-year-in-brazil-is-just-beginning/))
- Shopee has evolved from a cross-border import platform into a primarily **localized marketplace** with 3 million local sellers

### Shein

Shein entered Brazil in 2020 and rapidly became one of the most recognized fashion brands among Brazilian consumers. Key metrics:

- **Revenue in Brazil**: **US$3 billion in 2023** (7% of Shein's projected global sales of US$48 billion), per Coresight Research ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/retail-consumer/shein-tried-turn-brazil-into-production-hub-local-factories-walked-away-2026-02-04/))
- Brazil is Shein's **second-largest market globally after the US** as of 2025
- **2025 GMV in Brazil**: exceeded **BRL 15 billion** (US$~2.5 billion) ([NeoFeed](https://neofeed.com.br/negocios/nem-a-taxa-das-blusinhas-barrou-shopee-shein-temu-e-tiktok-shop-avancam-no-brasil/en/))
- **Local manufacturing effort**: In 2023, Shein pledged US$150 million to produce clothing in 2,000 Brazilian factories and create 100,000 jobs. By end of 2023, it had partnered with 336 factories, but the program largely collapsed as Shein demanded price cuts of up to 30% and accelerated delivery timelines beyond what local factories could manage ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/retail-consumer/shein-tried-turn-brazil-into-production-hub-local-factories-walked-away-2026-02-04/))
- Currently supports **over 45,000 local sellers** on its marketplace
- The 2024 "taxa das blusinhas" (blouse tax)—the informal name for the new 20% import duty on sub-US$50 parcels—was widely seen as a response to Shein's market penetration

### AliExpress (Alibaba)

- Brazil ranks as **AliExpress's third-largest market by country share**, accounting for **6.34% of total user traffic** globally (behind Spain at 7.68% and the US at 6.58%) ([GrabOn](https://grabon.com/blog/aliexpress-statistics/))
- AliExpress drove 25% of the growth in Alibaba's International Digital Commerce (AIDC) segment, which saw 22% year-on-year revenue growth in Q2 2025 ([GrabOn](https://grabon.com/blog/aliexpress-statistics/))
- Brazilian demand for AliExpress fell slightly in H1 2024 amid the new Remessa Conforme taxation ([Rio Times](https://www.riotimesonline.com/brazilian-e-commerce-flourishes-domestic-platforms-outshine-foreign-competitors/)), but the platform remains deeply embedded in Brazil's cross-border shopping culture

### Temu (PDD Holdings)

Temu entered Brazil in late 2024 and achieved explosive growth:

- Reached **39 million monthly active users** in Brazil after just six months of operation, a **600% increase** from its launch ([Rio Times](https://www.riotimesonline.com/temu-surpasses-mercado-livre-as-brazils-second-largest-marketplace/))
- In April 2025, **surpassed Shopee as the second most-visited e-commerce site** in Brazil, recording 56 million visits—9.9% of total e-commerce traffic—versus Mercado Livre at 12.3% ([ChineseSellers/Conversion](https://chinesellers.substack.com/p/temu-surpasses-shopee-as-brazils))
- Brazil's import e-commerce sector, fueled heavily by Temu, hit **450 million visits—up 132% year-on-year** ([ChineseSellers](https://chinesellers.substack.com/p/temu-surpasses-shopee-as-brazils))
- Temu averaged **114 million monthly active users across Latin America** in H1 2025, a 165% increase year-on-year ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/02/chinese-imports-latin-america-mexico-argentina/))
- BTG Pactual noted Temu's GMV figures for 2025 in the "billions of reais" in its first full year ([NeoFeed](https://neofeed.com.br/negocios/nem-a-taxa-das-blusinhas-barrou-shopee-shein-temu-e-tiktok-shop-avancam-no-brasil/en/))

## 6. Brazil vs. Other Latin American Countries as a Destination for Chinese Goods

Brazil is China's **largest trading partner in Latin America by far**, and Latin America as a whole has become increasingly important to China as US trade tensions escalate.

### Latin America as a Whole

In 2024, **China's trade with Latin America reached a record US$518 billion**, with predictions from some economists that it could exceed US$700 billion by 2034 ([Chatham House/Facebook post](https://www.facebook.com/ChathamHouse/posts/in-2024-chinas-trade-with-latin-america-grew-to-a-record-518bn-and-some-economis/1292670109564010/)). By November 2025, while Chinese exports to the US fell 18%, shipments to Latin American countries increased **nearly 8% to approximately US$276 billion** ([Mexico Business](https://mexicobusiness.news/trade-and-investment/news/china-expands-influence-latin-america-amid-us-tensions)).

### Country-by-Country Trade Deficits vs. Surpluses

| Country | 2024–2025 Trade Position with China |
|---------|--------------------------------------|
| **Brazil** | **Surplus** of ~US$29–44 billion (due to commodity exports) |
| **Mexico** | **Deficit** of ~US$120 billion (Jan–Oct 2025) |
| **Argentina** | **Deficit** of ~US$8.2 billion (2025) |
| **Chile** | **Surplus** (copper, lithium, fruit exports) |

*Sources: [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/02/chinese-imports-latin-america-mexico-argentina/), [AP News](https://apnews.com/article/china-latin-trump-trade-jinping-3bd62dd2d73ed711fa754ead1997634a)*

Brazil is one of the **very few Latin American countries** that maintains a trade surplus with China. This makes its political position on Chinese trade uniquely nuanced: it benefits from high commodity prices and Chinese demand while simultaneously facing competitive pressure from Chinese manufactured imports in sectors like steel, textiles, EVs, and consumer goods.

### EVs in Latin America

- Brazil: **>80% of EVs sold in 2024 were Chinese brands**; made Brazil the **second-largest global destination for Chinese cars after Russia** in 2024 ([AP News](https://apnews.com/article/china-latin-trump-trade-jinping-3bd62dd2d73ed711fa754ead1997634a))
- Mexico: **~15% market share** for Chinese vehicles in 2024; however, Mexico imposed tariffs of up to 50% on Chinese imports including EVs ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/02/chinese-imports-latin-america-mexico-argentina/))
- Mexico became the **single largest destination for Chinese auto exports globally in 2024**, importing 625,187 vehicles ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/02/02/chinese-imports-latin-america-mexico-argentina/))

### E-Commerce Comparison

Brazil has the most developed and competitive e-commerce market in Latin America, with the highest domestic spending and the deepest penetration of Chinese platforms. Argentina and Mexico also have significant Chinese platform presence but at lower absolute volumes.

### Chinese FDI in Latin America

China's Ministry of Commerce reported Chinese direct investment in Latin America reached **US$14.7 billion in 2024** ([Mexico Business](https://mexicobusiness.news/trade-and-investment/news/china-expands-influence-latin-america-amid-us-tensions)). Brazil remains the largest single recipient of Chinese FDI in the region, with cumulative Chinese investment exceeding US$70 billion since 2009 ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)). Key sectors include mining (iron ore), energy infrastructure, agribusiness logistics (port terminals), and telecommunications.

## 7. Recent Trade Agreements and Policy Changes (2024–2026)

### Xi Jinping's State Visit to Brazil (November 2024)

Chinese President Xi Jinping visited Brazil for the G20 summit and an official state visit in mid-November 2024. The two leaders **signed 37 bilateral agreements** on trade, technology, agriculture, solar energy, communications, and nuclear power ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/china/chinas-xi-visits-brasilia-cap-tour-flexing-diplomatic-clout-2024-11-20/), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/11/20/lula-and-xi-sign-dozens-of-trade-deals-as-brazil-china-ties-deepen/), [HKTDC Research](https://research.hktdc.com/en/article/MTg5OTE5NjM2OA)).

Key agreements included:
- **Agriculture access protocols**: New phytosanitary agreements opened the Chinese market to Brazilian fresh table grapes, sesame, sorghum, and fish derivatives; Brazilian Agriculture Ministry welcomed an estimated **US$450 million in new exports to China**
- **SpaceSail agreement**: China's low-Earth orbit satellite company SpaceSail signed an agreement with Brazilian telecom company Telebras to enter the Brazilian market
- **BNDES–China Development Bank loan**: Brazilian state bank BNDES arranged a **5 billion yuan (US$690 million)** loan from the China Development Bank, the first operation in Chinese currency
- Diplomatic relations elevated to **"China-Brazil Community of Shared Destiny for Building a More Just World and a More Sustainable Planet"**
- Brazil "recognized the relevance" of China's Belt and Road Initiative (while declining formal BRI membership)

### Double Tax Treaty Protocol (2024–2025)

Brazil's Chamber of Deputies approved a protocol amending the Brazil–China Double Tax Treaty in November 2024 (timed to Xi's visit), and the Brazilian Senate ratified it via **Legislative Decree No. 170/2025** in April 2025 ([International Tax Review](https://www.internationaltaxreview.com/article/2f1rrqkr6i2ss3uxakzr4/sponsored/brazil-ratifies-china-tax-treaty-changes-in-step-towards-modern-fiscal-alignment)).

Key changes:
- **Dividends**: Companies holding ≥25% capital stake may now be taxed at maximum **10%** (down from higher prior rates)
- **Royalties on trademarks**: Maximum rate reduced to **15%** (from 25%)
- **Royalties on other IP**: Maximum rate capped at **10%** (from 15%)
- Added **Principal Purpose Test** (OECD anti-abuse standard)
- Enhanced mutual agreement procedure for dispute resolution

The original treaty was signed in 1991. This update aligns the treaty with OECD/BEPS standards and is expected to reduce tax friction for Chinese FDI in Brazil's agribusiness, mining, and infrastructure sectors.

### Local Currency Settlement (March 2023)

In March 2023, Brazil and China agreed to **eliminate the US dollar as an intermediary currency** and settle bilateral trade directly in Brazilian reais and Chinese yuan ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)). This reduces foreign exchange risk and transaction costs for bilateral traders.

### Brazil's Automobile Import Tariffs

Brazil resumed a **10% automobile import tax in January 2023**, with a plan to gradually increase it to **35% by mid-2026** (confirmed by Decree No. 12.435, April 2025) ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)). This policy is driving Chinese automakers (BYD, GWM, Chery) to establish local Brazilian assembly operations to avoid the tariff.

Over 70,000 unsold Chinese electric vehicles reportedly piled up at Brazilian ports in late 2024 amid the tariff transition ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)).

### Textile Tariffs and Anti-Dumping (October 2024)

In October 2024, Brazil implemented new tariff policies and imposed **anti-dumping duties on many Chinese goods** in the textile sector, canceling prior favorable treatment ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)).

### US Tariff Fallout and Mercosur–China Discussions (2025–2026)

The Trump administration's imposition of tariffs in 2025 has indirectly deepened China–Brazil economic ties. In April 2025, Brazil's Vice President Alckmin held a video call with China's Commerce Minister Wang Wentao to explore expanding bilateral trade ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)). By February 2026, Reuters reported senior Brazilian officials were **warming to the idea of a limited Mercosur–China trade deal**, a significant shift given Brazil's prior resistance to such discussions ([Brazilian Report](https://newsletters.brazilian.report/p/mercosur-china-deal-tax-reform-rare-earths-trump)).

**Note:** Despite widespread social media claims, Brazil and China have **not signed a binding 20-year trade pact**. The Brazilian government's Communications Secretariat confirmed this explicitly in a statement to Reuters ([Reuters fact check](https://www.reuters.com/fact-check/brazil-china-have-not-signed-20-year-trade-pact-contrary-online-claims-2025-08-05/)).

## 8. The Remessa Conforme Program and Its Impact on Cross-Border E-Commerce Taxation

### Origins

Prior to 2023, Brazil's regulation of cross-border e-commerce packages was fragmented and poorly enforced. The standard rule applied a **flat 60% import duty** on all B2C imports under the Simplified Taxation Regime (RTS). However, gifts under US$50 from individuals were exempt, and only two of Brazil's 27 states were actually collecting ICMS on e-commerce imports. The result was widespread non-compliance, with less than 3.17% of e-commerce packages having registered customs declarations by 2022 ([WCO News](https://mag.wcoomd.org/magazine/wco-news-103/strengthening-e-commerce-compliance-overview-of-brazil-programa-remessa-conforme/)).

The Procomex E-Commerce Study Group (formed in 2020, representing 28 organizations covering 80%+ of the market) conducted 31 meetings between November 2021 and October 2023, including 13 sessions with the Brazilian Federal Revenue Service (Receita Federal do Brasil/RFB). A comprehensive report was delivered to the RFB in November 2022.

### Phase 1: Launch (August 2023)

The **Programa Remessa Conforme (PRC)** took effect on **August 1, 2023**, created under a voluntary compliance framework. Under Phase 1:

- E-commerce platforms that enrolled in the program were permitted to receive the benefit of **0% import duty** on shipments valued up to US$50 (CIF), in exchange for:
  - Pre-arrival registration of all Shipment Import Declarations (DIR) in the Siscomex Remessa system
  - Collection and remittance of **17% ICMS** (Brazil's state-level value-added tax) from buyers at checkout
  - Full tax transparency: displaying product origin, international freight, insurance, and all applicable taxes to consumers at checkout
  - Compliance with anti-smuggling standards and seller monitoring policies
- Non-enrolled platforms remained subject to the flat 60% import duty
- ICMS was standardized at 17% across all Brazilian states (previously only collected in 2 states)
- Compliant shipments received **prioritized customs clearance** (hours, not the prior 30 days) and reduced inspection rates

First international shipments under the PRC arrived in Brazil in late September 2023. The program's initial results were exceptional: tax collection in Aug–Dec 2023 was **222.91% higher** than the same period in 2022, with additional revenue of approximately **R$386 million (US$77 million)** ([WCO News](https://mag.wcoomd.org/magazine/wco-news-103/strengthening-e-commerce-compliance-overview-of-brazil-programa-remessa-conforme/)).

### Phase 2: Law 14,902/24 and August 2024 Changes

The **US$50 import duty exemption was eliminated** for PRC-enrolled platforms as of **August 1, 2024**, under Law 14,902/24 (conversion of Bill 914/24) ([Machado Meyer](https://www.machadomeyer.com.br/en/recent-publications/publications/tax/new-rules-for-the-remessa-conforme-program), [Passport Shipping](https://passportglobal.com/blog/brazils-ecommerce-compliance-program-prc-understanding-tax-advantages/)).

Post-August 2024 regime:

| Scenario | Import Duty | ICMS |
|----------|-------------|------|
| PRC-enrolled platform, shipment ≤ US$50 | **20%** | 17% on (value + II) |
| PRC-enrolled platform, shipment US$50.01–$3,000 | **60% minus US$20** | 17% |
| Non-PRC platform, any value | **60%** (no deduction) | 17% |
| Any shipment > US$3,000 | Formal customs clearance required | Variable |

The Brazilian government revoked the Ministry of Finance's authority to provide import duty exemptions for parcels valued up to US$100, eliminating any future regulatory workaround.

This change colloquially became known as the **"taxa das blusinhas"** (blouse tax) in Brazil, given that affordable Shein fashion items were the public face of the policy debate.

### Impact Assessment

**Fiscal impact**: Government tax collection from international parcels reached **R$2.88 billion in 2024**, up 45% from R$1.98 billion in 2023 ([Reddit/G1 Globo](https://www.reddit.com/r/AliExpressBR/comments/1lgbn8i/brasileiros_gastaram_quase_r_15_bilh%C3%B5es_em/)). However, platform revenues grew 140% over the same period, indicating that the tax increased costs but did not suppress demand.

**Volume impact**: The 2024 changes caused a "sharp drop in the volume of cross-border orders" in the short run, but spending in absolute terms still hit a new record of R$15 billion (190 million orders) in 2024 vs. R$6.42 billion in 2023 ([BTG Pactual as cited in NeoFeed](https://neofeed.com.br/negocios/nem-a-taxa-das-blusinhas-barrou-shopee-shein-temu-e-tiktok-shop-avancam-no-brasil/en/)).

**Logistics speed**: Brazil's Logistics Performance Index improved to **3.2 in 2024**, with the PRC cutting compliant parcel clearance from up to 30 days to hours ([World Bank LPI, cited by Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/brazil-courier-express-and-parcel-cep-market)).

**Platform adaptation**: All major Chinese platforms—Shopee, Shein, AliExpress, and Amazon—enrolled in the PRC or adopted PRC-compliant shipping models. Amazon Brazil announced in October 2024 that cross-border parcels must follow the PRC model. Platforms integrated tax calculators into checkout flows ([Amazon via SendFromChina](https://www.sendfromchina.com/NewsCenter/amazon-brazil-offer-commission-discounts.html)).

**Structural shift**: Brazil is transitioning from an incentive-and-regularization phase to more stringent enforcement. Authorities began issuing fines on parcels from Shopee and AliExpress that fall outside the compliance program or have mis-declared values ([BusinessWire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260129960153/en/Brazil-B2C-Ecommerce-Market-Report-2025-2029)). Price gaps between cross-border and domestic platforms on ultra-low-ticket items are expected to narrow as full tax compliance is built into pricing.

## 9. Structural Observations and Outlook

### The Commodity-Manufacturer Dynamic

Brazil's trade relationship with China replicates the classic developing-economy pattern: high-value commodities exported, high-value manufactures imported. Over **90% of Brazil's exports to China consist of primary commodities** (soybeans, mineral fuels, iron ore, wood pulp, meat) ([CEIC Data](https://info.ceicdata.com/ceic-article-how-chinese-trade-reshaped-brazil)). Brazil's Economic Complexity Index ranking fell from 25th in 1995 to 70th in 2021, while China rose from 46th to 18th over the same period ([IEDI](https://www.iedi.org.br/artigos/textos/english/letter_iedi_n_1292.html))—a widening gap that concerns Brazilian economists.

### Geopolitical Acceleration in 2025

US tariff policy under the Trump administration significantly accelerated China–Brazil economic integration in 2025. Chinese demand for Brazilian soybeans and beef surged as Beijing sought to reduce agricultural dependence on the US; Brazil's soybean exports to China accounted for **28.7% of total Brazilian exports** in 2025, and oil sales to China were 4.5 times greater than to the US ([The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/aseanplus/aseanplus-news/2026/01/15/brazil-china-trade-hits-record-us171bil-in-2025-as-us-tariffs-prompt-export-pivot)). In April 2025, Chinese importers bought 40 freighters carrying approximately 2.4 million tonnes of soybeans from Brazil in a single week ([China Law Vision](https://www.chinalawvision.com/2025/05/international-trade/opportunities-and-challenges-of-brazil-china-trade-under-the-impact-of-us-tariffs/)).

### Key Tensions

1. **Industrial protection vs. cheap imports**: Domestic Brazilian industries (steel, textiles, EVs, consumer goods) are lobbying intensively for anti-dumping measures and higher tariffs on Chinese goods. Brazil has already imposed auto import tariffs, textile anti-dumping duties, and e-commerce parcel taxes.
2. **Trade deal uncertainty**: A full Mercosur–China FTA would alarm Brazil's manufacturing base. As of early 2026, only a limited or partial deal is under discussion.
3. **Cross-border e-commerce enforcement**: The PRC regime has formalized and taxed the sector but has not halted the structural growth of Chinese platform penetration.
