# AI Industry Disruption

The AI transformation of the software industry is creating a K-shaped divergence between [[model-first-companies]] and everyone else, compressing timelines, destroying traditional moats, and forcing an identity crisis across the profession. [[source-ghuntley-collected|Geoffrey Huntley]] has been documenting this disruption since late 2024, making increasingly urgent calls for professionals to adapt.

## The Cost Collapse

Software development now costs **$10.42/hour** — less than minimum wage in most developed countries. When Huntley discovered [[ralph-loops|Ralph]] in early 2025, a private equity firm went short on Atlassian based on the implications and "made absolute bank."

## The K-Shape

Two diverging paths:
- **Model-first companies** — Lean apex predators operating on razor-thin margins, crushing incumbents
- **Traditional companies** — Need people transformation, AI strategy, and to deal with fundamentally changed business economics

As models improve at "slope-on-slope derivative pace," model-first companies close the gap faster and the cycle accelerates.

## The Teleporting Effect

AI enables people to "teleport to the future and rob your future self of retirement projects." Anything you've been putting off, you can build now. This creates:

- **Creative psychosis** — A 2-3 month period where builders can't stop creating because suddenly everything is possible
- **Entrepreneurial awakening** — People realizing they're "no longer dependent on others to achieve certain financial outcomes"
- **The chasm** — Those who've crossed it can't stop building; those who haven't may not make it

> "If you're having trouble sleeping because of all the things that you want to create, congratulations. You've made it through to the other side of the chasm."

## The Abundance Shift

Software moves from **scarcity** to **abundance**:
- Differentiated libraries → easily generated code
- Hard-to-replicate tech → screenshot + reimplement via AI
- Vendor lock-in → trivial auto-migration
- High switching costs → nonexistent

This challenges venture capital itself: "Why does someone need to raise a large amount of capital if it's just a five-person show now?"

## What Leaders Must Do

- Ambitious founders who understand AI can do anything → expand scope, don't lay off
- Lazy leaders without ambitious plans → forced to lay off as backlogs dry up
- Everyone: "Tap someone on the shoulder and stress to them to treat this topic seriously, upskill, and explain the risks"

> "For a lot of people, they haven't noticed AI is knocking on their door because AI is burrowing under their house."

## Timeline

- **Christmas 2024** — Huntley's "oh fuck moment." Models already good enough for disruption but required skill
- **Christmas 2025** — Mass "oh fuck moment." Models came "broken in and ready to get shit done"
- **March 2026** — Model-first companies arriving at incumbents' doors. Cost below minimum wage. VCs questioning their business model.
