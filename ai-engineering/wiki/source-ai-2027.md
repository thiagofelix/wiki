# Source: AI 2027

A research-backed scenario forecast by Daniel Kokotajlo (former OpenAI researcher, TIME100), Eli Lifland (#1 RAND Forecasting Initiative), Thomas Larsen (Center for AI Policy founder), and Romeo Dean (Harvard CS), with prose by Scott Alexander. Predicts superhuman AI impact exceeding the Industrial Revolution.

## Metadata

- **Authors:** Daniel Kokotajlo, Eli Lifland, Thomas Larsen, Romeo Dean, Scott Alexander
- **Source:** https://ai-2027.com/
- **Raw file:** `raw/AI 2027.md`
- **Published:** ~Nov 2025 (updated ongoing)
- **Endorsement:** Yoshua Bengio

## Key Claims

- AGI arrives around 2027 (modal prediction, median somewhat later)
- AI R&D progress multiplier reaches 1.5x by early 2026
- Training compute reaches 10^28 FLOP by late 2025 — 1,000x GPT-4
- Junior SWE job market in turmoil by late 2026
- Stock market up 30% in 2026, led by AI companies
- China nationalizes AI research mid-2026, creates centralized development zone
- Nation-state competition for AI weights escalates to potential military consideration
- Global AI capex reaches $1T

## Methodology

Informed by ~25 tabletop exercises, 100+ expert feedback, trend extrapolations, wargames, and experience at OpenAI. Written iteratively with two endings (slowdown vs race). Goal is predictive accuracy, not recommendation.

## Two Endings

- **Slowdown**: Coordinated international response, safety-first approach
- **Race**: Unchecked competition between US and China, escalating risks
