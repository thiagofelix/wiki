# Software Development vs. Software Engineering

In the AI era, [[source-ghuntley-collected|Geoffrey Huntley]] draws a sharp distinction between software development (typing code, prompting LLMs) and software engineering (designing safe, reliable systems and automations). Development has been commoditized and is accessible to anyone; engineering is more essential than ever and is evolving into a new discipline.

## Software Development (Commoditized)

- Typing code, prompting LLMs for output
- Accelerating massively — anyone can now be a "developer" (hairdressers using Cursor, non-developers at meetups showing off creations)
- The knowledge and skill of being a software developer has been commoditized
- Cost: $10.42/hour, less than minimum wage
- "Software development is dead — I killed it. Software can now be developed cheaper than the wage of a burger flipper."

## Software Engineering (Evolving)

Engineers now act like **locomotive engineers** — keeping the train on tracks, not shoveling coal:
- Designing loops, automations, safety mechanisms
- Sandboxing, credential management, security
- Risk engineering and responsible AI utilization
- Programming the loop rather than writing the code
- Building and tuning [[back-pressure]] mechanisms
- Designing [[software-factories]] and [[ralph-loops]]

> "If your employer bans AI tools, leave immediately: it's business suicide to ignore AI, while staying risks employability suicide as the market for manual coders shrinks rapidly."

## The Identity Crisis

AI erases traditional developer identities — backend, frontend, Ruby, Node.js. This creates emotional challenges for specialists with decades of experience. It extends to people managers too: "If your identity function is managing people, you need to make adjustments. Get back onto the tools ASAP."

## The Consumer vs. Builder Divide

From the "teleport" post: "The only question that remains is whether you are going to be a consumer of these tools or someone who understands them deeply and automates your job function?"

- **Consumer** — Uses AI tools to accelerate traditional workflows. This is now baseline for employment.
- **Builder** — Understands LLMs as programmable computers, builds agents, automates job functions. This is where the future is.

> "Go build yourself an agent and taste building in the recursive latent space."

## Open Source Is "Dead"

Traditional open-source libraries existed to ease hiring and share reusable code. Now:
- Generating code avoids maintainer burnout, abandoned projects, supply-chain attacks
- First-party generated code evolves faster with full control
- Exception: highly sensitive areas like PKI/SSL
