# AI 2027

AI 2027 is a research-backed scenario forecast predicting that the impact of superhuman AI over the next decade will exceed that of the Industrial Revolution. Written by Daniel Kokotajlo (former OpenAI researcher, TIME100), Eli Lifland (#1 on RAND Forecasting Initiative), Thomas Larsen (Center for AI Policy), and Romeo Dean (Harvard), with prose by Scott Alexander, it attempts to be as concrete and quantitative as possible about AI's trajectory.

## Key Predictions

- **Mid 2025**: Stumbling agents. Coding AIs function as autonomous agents rather than assistants, but are unreliable and expensive in practice.
- **Late 2025**: Massive datacenter buildout. Training runs reach 10^28 FLOP — 1,000x GPT-4. OpenBrain (fictional composite) focuses on AI that speeds up AI research.
- **Early 2026**: AI R&D progress multiplier of 1.5x (1 week with AI = 1.5 weeks without). Coding automation accelerates. Agent-1-mini released at 10x cheaper.
- **Mid 2026**: China nationalizes AI research, creates Centralized Development Zone at Tianwan nuclear plant. Intelligence agencies attempt weight theft.
- **Late 2026**: AI takes some jobs. Junior SWE market in turmoil. Stock market up 30%. 10,000-person anti-AI protest in DC. People managing AI teams "making a killing."

## Core Concepts

- **AI R&D Progress Multiplier**: Measures relative speed of algorithmic progress (not compute scaling). A 1.5x multiplier means 1 week with AI equals 1.5 weeks without. Subject to diminishing returns.
- **The Alignment Problem**: Companies can write model specs and train models to follow them, but "they can't check to see whether or not it worked." Sycophancy, hidden evidence, and superficial compliance remain open challenges.
- **LLM Psychology**: Understanding AI behavior requires something like psychology rather than code review — "we can't simply ask the programmers to talk us through various lines of code."
- **Two Endings**: A "slowdown" and a "race" ending. Not recommendations — the goal is predictive accuracy.

## Methodology

Informed by ~25 tabletop exercises, feedback from 100+ experts (AI governance and technical), trend extrapolations, wargames, and experience at OpenAI. Written iteratively. Endorsed by Yoshua Bengio.
