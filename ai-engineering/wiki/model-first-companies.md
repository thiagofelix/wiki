# Model-First Companies

Model-first companies are a new category of businesses where the majority of software is AI-generated, operations are highly automated, and the organization runs as a lean apex predator on razor-thin margins. [[source-ghuntley-collected|Geoffrey Huntley]] defines them as companies "building with the data ('grain') that has been baked into the large language models" — working with model weights rather than against them.

## Characteristics

- Software is generated, not hand-crafted
- Teams are radically small (20 people doing 30x the output of 60)
- Operations automated through [[ralph-loops]] and [[software-factories]]
- First-party everything — no dependency on SaaS stacks
- Utility-based pricing instead of per-seat pricing

## What Moats Aren't Anymore

Traditional software moats have been destroyed:
- **Per-seat pricing** — Model-first competitors operate leaner with utility pricing
- **Product features** — Can be screenshot'd and rebuilt with Claude Code ("window shopping on SaaS websites, rip a screenshot into Claude Code, and it rebuilds that product feature")
- **Switching costs / lock-in** — Migrations are trivial; Huntley migrated from Cloudflare D1 to PlanetScale Postgres automatically with a Ralph loop
- **Technical complexity** — AI commoditizes the ability to build complex software

## What Moats Are

- **Distribution** — Brand awareness, steaks and handshakes, relationships
- **Utility-based pricing** — Cents per megabyte, CPU hour
- **Speed of transformation** — Operating as model-first and accelerating
- **Taste and judgment** — Knowing what to build (and what not to)
- **Contracts and relationships** — "The hard things of business"

## The K-Shape Economy

The world is dividing into:
1. **Model-first companies** — Lean, fast, crushing incumbents
2. **Everyone else** — Needs people transformation, figuring out AI, dealing with changed fundamentals

As models improve (slope-on-slope derivative pace), model-first companies get better at automating, arrive at incumbents' doors in months not years, and the "snake eating its tail speeds up."

## The LLM Weight Grain

From the "papercuts" post: these companies are "model weight first" — building with the grain of what LLMs are good at, rather than fighting against their tendencies. This means choosing architectures, patterns, and even programming languages that align with what the models produce well.
