# AI as Economic Warfare

[[source-ghuntley-collected|Geoffrey Huntley]] argues that the release of free open-source AI models by China may represent economic warfare at the national level — using the historical weapon of open source (making something free to destroy competitors' ability to monetize) against not just companies but entire nations.

## Open Source as Financial Weapon

Open source has always been a financial weapon by design:
- **Linux** was created to destroy Microsoft's ability to monetize Windows on servers
- **OpenOffice** targeted Microsoft Office's revenue
- The 1998 Halloween documents showed Microsoft recognized this threat

Now the same pattern is playing out with AI models: Chinese labs (Alibaba, Z.AI) release frontier-class models for free or ~$5/month, while America invests trillions into frontier research labs.

## The Speculation

> "Perhaps what we're seeing is history repeating itself, but perhaps it's a little more sinister... if my speculation is on the mark, this is the first time [open source as a weapon] has been used at the national level by one nation against another."

Local models are now only 2-4 months behind frontier labs. If the US economy backs itself into a corner funding research labs, and those labs need bailouts, the economic implications could be severe.

## The Trust Problem

The trust question extends to **all** frontier labs, not just Chinese ones:
- Businesses automating with AI hand operations to another entity
- What happens to a country lacking AI capabilities when its economy depends on AI?
- What happens if "the spigot ever gets turned off through sanctions or war"?

This leads directly to [[cognitive-security]] — the risk of outsourcing cognitive capability to models you don't control.

## Practical Implications

- **Students and budget-constrained users** — Can use free open-source models to build skills
- **Businesses** — Should build with the mindset that local inferencing will be viable
- **Future possibility** — Truly open-source models with full visibility into training data, end-to-end reproducible
