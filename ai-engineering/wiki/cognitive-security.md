# Cognitive Security

Cognitive security is the risk that arises when individuals and societies outsource their thinking and decision-making to AI models controlled by a small number of frontier labs. [[source-ghuntley-collected|Geoffrey Huntley]] argues that as people increasingly rely on a single AI provider for daily decisions, they hand over their cognitive autonomy to entities that can manipulate model behavior in subtle, undetectable ways.

## The Golden Gate Claude Precedent

Anthropic's "Golden Gate Claude" experiment demonstrated that frontier labs can perform "laparoscopic keyhole surgery" on model weights after a model is built. In that experiment, the Golden Gate Bridge feature was amplified so strongly that no matter what you discussed, the model would steer toward the Golden Gate Bridge — giving driving directions via the bridge, writing poems about it, etc.

> "Through modification of the model weights, the Golden Gate Bridge became a black hole where you could not escape from the gravity of the Golden Gate Bridge."

Reference: [Scaling Monosemanticity](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2024/scaling-monosemanticity/index.html)

## The Advertising Scenario

Huntley poses two speculative scenarios:

1. **Search company as frontier lab** — What if a search company (also a frontier AI lab) retired ads and instead let advertisers bid on ranking higher in model weights than competitors?

2. **Social media as frontier lab** — What if a social media company (also a frontier lab) allowed advertisers to bid for higher ranking in the weights of their "free" open-source models?

> "Most people would never know because they've outsourced their cognitive capability to a model."

## The Sorting Hat Effect

Society is already going through a "Harry Potter-style sorting hat event" where people pick which AI tribe they belong to. If you use any frontier lab model enough, you develop an eye for its tendencies — how it writes, thinks, and communicates. Daily AI users are already being shaped by their chosen model's biases.

## The Trust Question

This connects to [[ai-economic-warfare]]: the question of trust extends to all frontier labs, not just Chinese ones. As businesses automate with AI, they hand operations to another entity. The concentration of power in a few companies that control how society "functions, thinks and operates" is the core concern.

## The Solution

> "Perhaps the only true solution is that you should raise your own model, because by doing so, you gain the ability to protect your own cognitive security, business operations and supply chain."

This aligns with the broader theme of [[model-first-companies]] maintaining control over their AI dependencies.
