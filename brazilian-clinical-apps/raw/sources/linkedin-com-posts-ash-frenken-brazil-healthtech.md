15 years building. A new country. And a thesis that keeps getting stronger.

I've spent my career drawn to the same thing, real problems, real people, and technology that genuinely closes the gap between the two. The Australian startup ecosystem gave me 15 years of hard lessons: the pivots, the "why would anyone fund this?" moment... and let's not forget the countless moment of self-doubt...but it was the quiet satisfaction of watching something you built actually matter that keeps you coming back for more.

Last year, I moved to Brazil with no network... zero... nada. What I found surprised me.

Over the last few months, I've immersed myself in conversations with founders and VCs across São Paulo and Rio, and I keep walking away with the same feeling: this ecosystem isn't just growing, it's maturing... and fast. Brazilian startups raised a record $10.5 billion in 2024! 🇧🇷🚀

World-class businesses are being built here for global markets, by founders operating with genuine ambition. A country not without its challenges, but rapidly becoming one of the world's real innovation powerhouses.
For years, Brazil's startup story has been told through largely a fin-tech lens... And rightfully so. Brazil accounts for 58.7% of all fintechs in Latin America and produced some of the world's most compelling case studies in financial inclusion (Aussies, check out Nubank's story for something that'll knock your socks off). But a new chapter is being written ... and it's about health. A recent IT Forum piece (link below) captures what the data has been signalling: healthtech is Brazil's next frontier. Investment surpassed R$2 billion in 2024, up 18% year-on-year, with over 1,200 active healthtechs now operating across the country! A chronically under-resourced public health system serving 215 million people. 30 million+ telemedicine consultations in 2024 alone. AI adoption accelerating. Capital following conviction. Brazil's fintech revolution was fundamentally about access, taking a broken, exclusionary system and opening it up through smart technology. That playbook translates to health almost perfectly. A decade of fintech infrastructure has quietly laid the groundwork for something much bigger. The question isn't whether health-tech defines Brazil's next chapter, it's who builds it, and whether they build it with the scale this country's people actually deserve. I have some thoughts on that. More to come. 👀 👀 👀 #Brazil #Healthtech #Startups #LatAm #Innovation #FounderJourney https://lnkd.in/dvnHYYRS

Brasil vai de país das fintechs ao país das healthtechs em 2025 https://itforum.com.br
