## MV expands its operations and announces its entry into the United States market.

29/10/2025

					Share

Solutions for patient journey management, telemedicine, and AI will be implemented. in clinics in Florida

MV, a leader in healthcare technology in Latin America and currently recognized as the 5th largest provider of hospital software in the world, announces its entry into the North American market through a partnership with the Medstation group.

This initiative marks a decisive step in the company&#039;s internationalization process and brings digital healthcare journey solutions to the US public, such as MV Clinic and the Global Health platform, which integrate clinical data and enhance patient-centered care.

Launched in September, the operation will have a dedicated internationalization team, supported by local partners to handle all necessary regulations. Over the next two years, approximately US$$10 million will be invested in the project, with innovations that will also have an impact in Brazil.

MV&#039;s cutting-edge technology is enhanced by integration with Artificial Intelligence solutions from the startup Sofya, which recently received investments from the company. AI will be integrated into electronic health record systems to provide clinical decision support and optimize medical routines.

The move reinforces the company&#039;s global strategy. In March 2025, during HIMSS in Las Vegas, MV presented its technologies fully adapted for the American market, receiving great receptivity from the sector. “We had been testing the North American market for some time and identified an important gap in the US healthcare sector that can be filled with the experience and innovations we have developed in Brazil. Being present in this market, which is the most competitive and challenging in the world, is an essential step to consolidate our global position,” explains Paulo Magnus, CEO of MV.

In the initial phase of the partnership with Medstation, solutions will be made available for patient care and clinical journeys, telemedicine, and artificial intelligence. The expectation is to expand MV&#039;s technology portfolio to other areas of the healthcare system in the future.

“We are building a bridge between two healthcare ecosystems that can learn a lot from each other. The investment in the United States will strengthen our international presence and bring valuable lessons for us to continue innovating in Brazil as well,” adds Magnus.

Currently, MV operates in 11 Latin American countries, with an office in Panama, and also has clients in Angola. Entering the North American market consolidates the company&#039;s global expansion strategy and positions the brand as an international benchmark in innovation and technology for healthcare.

## Promotion for companies and workers affected by floods in RS

## 1. Financing Line via BNDES:

- 

Amount: R$ 15 billion.

- objective: Support the economic recovery of companies in Rio Grande do Sul.

- Conditions: Reduced interest rates and extended terms.

- Access: Financial institutions accredited by BNDES, including commercial banks, credit cooperatives and development agencies.

- Source: Ministry of Finance (Services and Information from Brazil).

## 2. Pronampe for Cooperatives:

- 

Amount: Part of the extraordinary credit of R$ 4.5 billion.

- objective: Facilitate access to credit for small and medium-sized companies, including cooperatives.

- Conditions: Competitive interest rates and guarantees by the Operations Guarantee Fund (FGO).

- Access: Banks participating in Pronampe.

- Source: Ministry of Entrepreneurship and Finance (Services and Information from Brazil).

## 3. BNDES Solidarity Credit Program:

- 

Amount: R$ 1 billion.

- objective: Assist rural producers, MEIs, micro, small and medium-sized companies.

- Conditions: Zero interest (corrected by IPCA), payment term of up to 60 months with a 24-month grace period.

- Access: Financial institutions accredited by BNDES.

- Source: BNDES.

## 4. Financial Assistance to Workers:

- 

Benefit: Payment of two installments of the minimum wage (R$ 1,412.00) for formal workers, domestic workers, interns and artisanal fishermen.

- Conditions: Companies need to join the program and keep workers employed for at least four months.

- Access: Through companies joining the program.

- Source: Ministry of Labor and Employment (Services and Information from Brazil).

## 5. Suspension of FGTS Collection:

- 

objective: Relieve companies&#039; cash flow.

- Conditions: Suspension of FGTS collection for four months.

- Access: Automatically available to businesses in affected areas.

- Source: Ministry of Labor and Employment (Services and Information from Brazil).

## 6. FGTS Calamity Withdrawal and Salary Allowance Advance:

- 

Amount: More than R$ 1.4 billion for calamity withdrawal and R$ 792 million for advance salary bonus.

- objective: Provide financial assistance to affected workers.

- Conditions: According to specific regulations for disasters.

- Access: Through Caixa Econômica Federal branches.

- Source: Ministry of Labor and Employment (Services and Information from Brazil).

## Brazil Movement, Digital Country

Brasil, País Digital is a movement led by the Brazilian Association of Software Companies (ABES) created in 2016, which has the support of various organizations in society. The objective of the movement is to work for the construction of a Nation Project that is more digital and less unequal, sharing information and promoting the debate on inclusive digital transformation.

In addition to promoting debates on inclusive and structuring public policies, the Movimento Brasil, País Digital has also been working hard to facilitate the Brazilian population’s access to basic concepts of digital transformation, the transformations that different sectors are going through, as well as the main risks and good practices for preventing cyber risks. Another relevant field of action has been to encourage the full exercise of citizenship in the Digital Age by sharing knowledge and promoting debates on the education of children and adolescents, positive leadership and responsible action in the networks, as well as digitization and access to public services through different channels. fingerprints.

The movement also pays tribute to those who make a difference in the country&#039;s digital transformation, contributing to progress in building a more digital and less unequal country. Thus, the Ambassador or Ambassador of the Brazil, Country Digital Movement award was born. In 2021, we launched the Protagonists award, a new category for recognizing personalities who strive, but do not always receive due recognition for their actions on behalf of the country and the community.

This curatorial work positively impacts Brazil, helping to meet SDGs 9 and 11.

## Mobilization for Inequality Reduction

“Mobilization to reduce inequality” is the name of the campaign launched by ABES, in partnership with ReUrbi and the Third Sector Observatory, with the support of the Weber Shandwick agency. It aims to support social inclusion projects that promote training in the digital area, through the resources obtained by recycling IT equipment (notebooks, PCs, racks, etc.) discarded by companies that adhere to the Program, which collects, recycling and reinserting equipment into the production chain.

Therefore, the Mobilization meets 8 of the SDGs, including quality education, reduction of inequalities and responsible consumption and production.
