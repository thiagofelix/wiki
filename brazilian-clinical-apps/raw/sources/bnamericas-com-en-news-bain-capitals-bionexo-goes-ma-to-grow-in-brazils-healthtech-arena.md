##  Bain Capital's Bionexo bets on M&A to grow in Brazil's healthtech arena 
  Bnamericas  Published: Friday, March 06, 2026     ERP   Mergers & Acquisitions   Databases   Innovation   Software Development Company               
Digital health solutions group Bionexo, invested by Bain Capital, has completed the acquisition of Tucano do Brasil Sistemas de Informação Ltda, a company belonging to the Philips conglomerate, to expand its presence in the healthtech sector, according to information obtained by BNamericas.
 
Founded in 2000, Bionexo has received significant investments, including contributions from BNDES for the development of artificial intelligence, and counts Bain Capital as one of its main shareholders.
 
The company develops and offers digital solutions for process management in the healthcare field.
 
Among its activities, it operates a digital business-to-business (“B2B”) marketplace platform that intermediates the process of buying and selling healthcare products between manufacturers, distributors, and private healthcare institutions, whether for-profit or non-profit.
 
The healthtech also offers additional digital solutions, such as tools for automating purchasing workflows and managing inventories and hospital revenue.
 
In addition, it provides software for clinic management with modules that cover everything from appointment scheduling to billing processes, as well as data intelligence tools that enable clients to operate more strategically in the market.
 
In 2021, Bain Capital announced an initial investment of 440 million (mn) reais and came to hold around 43% of Bionexo. At the time, the healthtech was valued at more than 1 billion (bn) reais.
 
The investment was aimed at supporting the company’s expansion in Brazil and Latin America, as well as strengthening its position in the health technology market.
 
Tucano, in turn, develops the Tasy (ERP/EMR) hospital management software, an electronic medical record platform, which will be incorporated into Bionexo’s portfolio.
 
Market
 
A recent study by the Brazilian startups association, Abstartups, indicates that, in 2025, the number of healthtechs operating in Brazil surpassed that of fintechs.
 
The sector has become the second strongest in the startup ecosystem, behind only edtechs (education technology startups).
 
Healthtechs account for 9.4% of the total 3,650 startups mapped in the country, equivalent to 343 active companies. The Abstartups survey was conducted between July and September 2025.
 
According to the HealthTech Recap 2024 report, produced by the Distrito platform for the Brazilian Association of Health Startups and Healthtechs (ABSS), Brazil already housed by the end of that year 602 health startups, equivalent to 64.8% of the total in Latin America.
 
According to the study, health startups in Latin America received more than US$1.5 billion (bn) in investments between 2014 and 2024.
