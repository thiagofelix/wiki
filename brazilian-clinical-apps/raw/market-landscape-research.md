## 3. Key Regulations Affecting Clinical Software

### 3.1 CFM — Telemedicine & Digital Health

**Governing body:** Conselho Federal de Medicina (CFM) — an autarchic federal body established by Law 3.268/1957.

#### Regulatory Timeline

| Year | Instrument | Key Change |
|---|---|---|
| 2002 | CFM Resolution 1.643/2002 | First telemedicine framework; limited to emergencies or at the attending physician's request |
| 2018 | CFM Resolution 2.227/2018 | Broader regulation covering teleconsulta, telecirurgia, telediagnóstico, etc. — later revoked amid controversy |
| 2020 | Ministry of Health Ordinance 467/2020 + Law 13.989/2020 | Emergency authorization of telemedicine during COVID-19 pandemic |
| 2022 (April) | **CFM Resolution 2.314/2022** | Current permanent regulation; replaces all prior telemedicine rules |
| 2022 (Dec) | **Law 14.510/2022** (Telehealth Act) | Federal law permanently authorizing telehealth across all health professions |
| 2022 | Ministry of Health Ordinance 1.348/2022 | Telehealth within SUS framework |
| 2024 | Ministry of Health Ordinance 3.691/2024 | "Digital SUS Strategy Action — Telehealth" |
| 2026 (Feb) | **CFM Resolution 2.454/2026** | AI in medicine — enters force August 10, 2026 |

#### CFM Resolution 2.314/2022 — Key Requirements for Software/Platform Providers

1. **Seven recognized modalities:** Teleconsulta, Teleinterconsulta, Telediagnóstico, Telecirurgia, Telemonitoramento/televigilância, Teletriagem, Teleconsultoria. ([AMIB](https://amib.org.br/resoluc%CC%A7a%CC%83o-no-2-314-de-20-de-abril-de-2022/))

2. **Patient informed consent:** Must be captured before each telehealth encounter; includes consent to data transmission. ([CMS Law](https://cms.law/en/int/expert-guides/cms-expert-guide-to-digital-health-apps-and-telemedicine/brazil))

3. **Electronic Health Record (SRES):** Anamnesis, diagnostic data, and medical conduct must be recorded in the patient's SRES. The system must support Level of Security Assurance 2 (NGS2) under ICP-Brasil (Brazilian Public Key Infrastructure) or another legally accepted standard. ([CMS Law](https://cms.law/en/int/expert-guides/cms-expert-guide-to-digital-health-apps-and-telemedicine/brazil))

4. **Digital signatures:** Remote prescriptions and reports require a physician's digital signature meeting ICP-Brasil standards. ([CMS Law](https://cms.law/en/int/expert-guides/cms-expert-guide-to-digital-health-apps-and-telemedicine/brazil))

5. **Territorial requirement:** Telemedicine service providers (companies) must be headquartered in Brazil and registered with the CRM (Regional Council of Medicine) of the state where they operate. ([Doctoralia](https://pro.doctoralia.com.br/blog/clinicas/telemedicina-o-que-diz-a-lei-brasileira))

6. **Physician autonomy:** The physician decides whether to use telemedicine or insist on in-person; this cannot be contractually removed. ([CFM via CREMAM](https://cremam.org.br/cfm-publica-normas-para-realizacao-da-telemedicina-no-brasil/))

7. **Data storage and security:** Services must follow CFM rules on storage, handling, integrity, accuracy, confidentiality, privacy, and professional secrecy. ([CMS Law](https://cms.law/en/int/expert-guides/cms-expert-guide-to-digital-health-apps-and-telemedicine/brazil))

8. **Chronic disease follow-up:** Patients with chronic conditions using telehealth must have an in-person visit at least every 180 days. ([AMB](https://amb.org.br/cfm-regulamenta-consultas-de-telemedicina-no-brasil/))

9. **Supervision:** Compliance is enforced by the respective state CRM. ([Doctoralia](https://pro.doctoralia.com.br/blog/clinicas/telemedicina-o-que-diz-a-lei-brasileira))

#### CFM Resolution 2.454/2026 — AI in Medicine (effective August 10, 2026)

- AI tools expressly permitted as **support instruments** for clinical decision-making, health management, research, and continuing education. ([JD Supra](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/brazilian-cfm-issues-resolution-on-the-6731720/))
- **Mandatory human supervision** — AI cannot replace the physician's final authority over clinical decisions. ([Machado Meyer](https://www.machadomeyer.com.br/pt/inteligencia-juridica/publicacoes-ij/life-sciences-e-saude/cfm-regulamenta-o-uso-de-inteligencia-artificial-na-medicina))
- Institutions deploying AI must implement: (a) continuous auditing and monitoring; (b) a dedicated **AI and Telemedicine Committee** under medical direction; (c) risk classification of AI tools (Low / Medium / High); (d) transparent reporting. ([Medicina S/A](https://medicinasa.com.br/eval-ia-corporativa/))
- **Physicians' right of refusal:** Any physician can decline to follow AI recommendations without penalty. ([Machado Meyer](https://www.machadomeyer.com.br/pt/inteligencia-juridica/publicacoes-ij/life-sciences-e-saude/cfm-regulamenta-o-uso-de-inteligencia-artificial-na-medicina))
- **Documentation requirement:** Use of AI in clinical decision-making must be recorded in the patient's medical record. ([CFM Resolution 2.454/2026 PDF](https://sistemas.cfm.org.br/normas/arquivos/resolucoes/BR/2026/2454_2026.pdf))
- Compliance window: **180 days** from publication (August 10, 2026). ([JD Supra](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/brazilian-cfm-issues-resolution-on-the-6731720/))

### 3.2 LGPD — Health Data Protection

**Governing instrument:** Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados Pessoais — Law 13.709/2018  
**Enforcement authority:** ANPD (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados)  
**Effective date:** August 2020 (penalties enforceable from August 2021)

#### Classification of Health Data

Health data is classified as **"dados pessoais sensíveis"** (sensitive personal data) under LGPD Article 5, XII, and Article 11. This classification triggers stricter processing rules than ordinary personal data. ([LGPD Brasil Info](https://lgpd-brasil.info/capitulo_02/artigo_11))

#### Legal Bases for Processing Health Data (Article 11)

Processing of sensitive health data is permitted only for specific purposes, including:
- **Explicit consent** of the data subject
- Performance of a legal or regulatory obligation
- Shared processing by public bodies for legally mandated public policies
- Studies by research bodies (with anonymization wherever possible)
- Protection of life or physical safety of the data subject or third party
- **Health protection** — exclusively by health professionals, health services, or sanitary authorities
- Prevention of fraud and security of the data subject

**Critical restriction:** Health insurers (operadoras de planos privados) are **explicitly prohibited** from using health data for risk selection in contracting or exclusion of beneficiaries (Article 11, §5). ([LGPD Brasil Info](https://lgpd-brasil.info/capitulo_02/artigo_11))

#### Key Compliance Requirements for Clinical Software

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Data mapping** | Catalog all personal data flows (including paper records) |
| **Legal basis documentation** | Each processing activity must have documented legal basis |
| **Consent management** | Granular, explicit, revocable consent; timestamp logs required |
| **DPO appointment** | Companies must appoint a Data Protection Officer (Encarregado) |
| **Data subject rights** | 15-day response window for access, correction, deletion requests |
| **Breach notification** | Report to ANPD within 3 business days of serious breach |
| **Encryption** | All patient data storage and transmission must be encrypted |
| **Cross-controller sharing** | Health data cannot be shared between controllers for economic advantage, except for healthcare delivery, pharmacy, and financial/administrative transactions arising from that care |
| **Retention** | Medical records must be retained ≥20 years (physical) or permanently (digital) per CFM Resolution 1.821/2007 |
| **Third-party processor contracts** | Written data processing agreements with all vendors; controllers share liability for processor failures |
| **DPIA** | Data Protection Impact Assessments recommended (though not explicitly mandated) for high-risk processing |

**Penalties:**  
- Fines up to **2% of Brazilian revenue, capped at R$50 million per infraction**  
- Suspension of data processing operations  
- Public disclosure of the violation  
([Portal Telemedicina](https://portaltelemedicina.com.br/lgpd-na-saude-como-garantir-a-seguranca-de-dados-dos-pacientes), [LGPD Compliance Guide](https://secureprivacy.ai/blog/lgpd-compliance-requirements))

#### LGPD and Healthcare-Specific Context

- **Whatsapp:** Permitted for patient communication if encrypted, but uncontrolled persistent messages remain risky and must meet LGPD requirements. ([Portal Telemedicina](https://portaltelemedicina.com.br/lgpd-na-saude-como-garantir-a-seguranca-de-dados-dos-pacientes))
- **TISS billing:** Billing data sent to insurers generally does not require separate consent; detailed clinical data in reports may require consent. ([Portal Telemedicina](https://portaltelemedicina.com.br/lgpd-na-saude-como-garantir-a-seguranca-de-dados-dos-pacientes))
- **Clinical software certification:** SBIS (Sociedade Brasileira de Informática em Saúde) evaluations are a recognized method to validate software security for health data. ([Portal Telemedicina](https://portaltelemedicina.com.br/lgpd-na-saude-como-garantir-a-seguranca-de-dados-dos-pacientes))
- Brazil led global data breach rankings in 2023, intensifying regulator focus on the health sector. ([Pixeon Blog](https://www.pixeon.com/blog/lgpd-na-saude/))

### 3.3 TISS — Health Insurance Interchange Standard

**Full name:** Troca de Informações na Saúde Suplementar  
**Established by:** ANS (Agência Nacional de Saúde Suplementar)  
**Governing resolution:** RN nº 501 (and subsequent updates)  
**Current version:** March 2024 ([ANS — Padrão TISS Março/2024](https://www.gov.br/ans/pt-br/assuntos/prestadores/padrao-para-troca-de-informacao-de-saude-suplementar-2013-tiss/marco-2024))

#### What It Is

TISS is the **mandatory electronic data exchange standard** for health plan beneficiary care data among health insurers (operadoras), healthcare providers (prestadores), and ANS. Every clinical software that interfaces with private health insurers in Brazil must be TISS-compliant. ([ANS](https://www.ans.gov.br/prestadores/tiss-troca-de-informacao-de-saude-suplementar))

#### Five Components of TISS

| Component | Description | Current Version |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Organizational** | Operational rules for who must comply and how | 202403 |
| **2. Content & Structure** | XML-based data architecture for electronic messages (guias) | 202211 |
| **3. Concepts Representation** | TUSS terminology for clinical codes and procedures | 202403 |
| **4. Security & Privacy** | Encryption and confidentiality requirements | 202305 |
| **5. Communication** | XML messaging formats, electronic protocols | 04.01.00 and 01.04.01 |

([ANS TISS March 2024](https://www.gov.br/ans/pt-br/assuntos/prestadores/padrao-para-troca-de-informacao-de-saude-suplementar-2013-tiss/marco-2024))

#### Covered Transactions

TISS standardizes electronic exchanges for:
- Authorization requests (guias de consultas, exames, internações, terapias)
- Billing/invoicing from providers to insurers
- Reimbursement claims by beneficiaries
- Monitoring data sent from insurers to ANS

**Key rule:** Insurers cannot request paper copies of information already transmitted electronically via TISS with a valid ICP-Brasil digital certificate. ([TISS Organizational Component PDF](https://www.gov.br/ans/pt-br/assuntos/prestadores/padrao-para-troca-de-informacao-de-saude-suplementar-2013-tiss/PadroTISS_ComponenteOrganizacional_202401.pdf))

**Interoperability mandate:** TISS must be interoperable with SUS systems (SIH, SIA, SIM, SINASC) and ANS registration systems. ([TISS Organizational Component PDF](https://www.gov.br/ans/pt-br/assuntos/prestadores/padrao-para-troca-de-informacao-de-saude-suplementar-2013-tiss/PadroTISS_ComponenteOrganizacional_202401.pdf))

#### Coordinator TISS

Each insurer must designate a "Coordenador TISS" — a technical professional responsible for TISS compliance and maintaining the corporate portal. ([ANS](https://www.ans.gov.br/prestadores/tiss-troca-de-informacao-de-saude-suplementar))

### 3.4 TUSS — Unified Health Terminology

**Full name:** Terminologia Unificada da Saúde Suplementar  
**Created by:** ANS + AMB (Associação Médica Brasileira) + COPISS (Comitê de Padronização das Informações em Saúde Suplementar)  
**Based on:** CBHPM 6th Edition (Classificação Brasileira Hierarquizada de Procedimentos Médicos)

#### What It Is

TUSS is the **standardized procedure code and nomenclature system** for the supplementary health sector. It is the "component 3" of TISS (concepts representation) and defines the codes used in all TISS electronic messages. ([ANS](https://www.ans.gov.br/a-ans/sala-de-noticias-ans/operadoras-e-servicos-de-saude/2010-rol-de-procedimentos-e-terminologia-unificada-da-saude-suplementar))

#### Five TUSS Categories

1. Medical procedures and other assistive services
2. Daily rates and service fees (diárias e taxas)
3. Materials and medications
4. Orthotics, prosthetics, and special materials (OPME)
5. Items not in CBHPM but recognized by ANS

([Mais Laudo](https://maislaudo.com.br/blog/tabela-tuss/))

#### Governance

- ANS maintains and updates TUSS; COPISS proposes additions and reviews codes.
- For unlisted items, an insurer can create a temporary proprietary code until COPISS approves inclusion. ([Mais Laudo](https://maislaudo.com.br/blog/tabela-tuss/))

**TISS vs TUSS:**  
- **TISS** = the electronic interchange standard (the "pipe")  
- **TUSS** = the terminology/codes used within that pipe (the "language")  
([Mais Laudo](https://maislaudo.com.br/blog/tabela-tuss/))

### 3.5 ANS Requirements

**ANS (Agência Nacional de Saúde Suplementar)** regulates Brazil's supplementary health market — roughly 668 active insurers (operadoras) covering ~51 million beneficiaries. ([ITA / Trade.gov](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-healthcare))

#### Key ANS Obligations Relevant to Clinical Software

| Obligation | Description |
|---|---|
| **TISS compliance** | All electronic data exchanges between providers and insurers must use TISS. Established by RN 501. |
| **DIOPS reporting** | Insurers submit quarterly/annual financial data via DIOPS-DOCS system (XML format). Established by RN 527. ([Blendus Blog](https://blendus.com.br/blog/normas-ans-para-operadoras-de-saude/)) |
| **Corporate portal** | Each insurer must maintain an internet-facing portal for TISS data exchange |
| **IDSS performance index** | ANS scores insurers on Índice de Desempenho da Saúde Suplementar; scores affect regulatory eligibility (e.g., certification programs). ([BVS](https://bvsms.saude.gov.br/bvs/saudelegis/ans/2025/res_0630_07_04_2025.html)) |
| **APS Certification** | Optional primary care certification (RN 572/2023); requires specific software capabilities and team composition tracking |
| **Rol de Procedimentos** | Mandatory minimum coverage list; clinical software billing modules must map against Rol codes |

### 3.6 ANVISA — SaMD (Software as Medical Device)

**ANVISA** is Brazil's health surveillance agency (equivalent to the FDA). As of 2022, it has established a dedicated regulatory framework for software.

#### Key Regulatory Instruments

| Resolution | Content |
|---|---|
| **RDC 657/2022** | Core SaMD regulation — defines SaMD, classification, exemptions, registration requirements, labeling, lifecycle, cybersecurity. Effective July 1, 2022. |
| **RDC 751/2022** | General medical device risk classification (Classes I–IV, 22 rules). Rule 11 specifically governs SaMD. Replaces RDC 185/2001. |
| **RDC 777/2023** | Updates and complements RDC 751/2022. |
| **RDC 848/2024** | Essential safety and performance requirements for medical devices and IVDs. |
| **RDC 665/2022** | Brazilian GMP (Good Manufacturing Practices) — required for Classes III/IV. |

Sources: [Elendiabs](https://www.elendilabs.com/en/articles/bra-software-as-a-medical-device), [CGM Law](https://cgmlaw.com.br/en/anvisa-resolution-no-657-2022-standard-for-registration-and-notification-of-software-as-a-medical-device/), [ANVISA.gov.br](https://www.gov.br/anvisa/pt-br/assuntos/noticias-anvisa/2025/anvisa-publica-nova-versao-de-manual-para-regularizacao-de-equipamentos-medicos)

#### What Is (and Is Not) SaMD

**SaMD includes:** Any software intended for medical purposes (prevention, diagnosis, treatment, rehabilitation, contraception) that operates independently of hardware — including mobile apps, SaaS platforms, and hosted services.

**Explicitly excluded from SaMD regulation:**
1. Software for well-being only
2. Software in ANVISA's non-regulated list
3. **Software used exclusively for administrative and financial management** in health services
4. Software that processes demographic/epidemiological data without clinical/diagnostic/therapeutic purpose
5. Software embedded in a registered hardware medical device

([RDC 657/2022 English PDF](https://www.gov.br/anvisa/pt-br/assuntos/produtosparasaude/temas-em-destaque/arquivos/2024/rdc-657-2022-en.pdf), [CMS Law](https://cms.law/en/int/expert-guides/cms-expert-guide-to-digital-health-apps-and-telemedicine/brazil))

#### SaMD Risk Classification (RDC 751/2022, Rule 11)

| Class | Risk Level | Examples | Pathway |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Class I** | Low | Admin-supporting software, wellness monitoring with no clinical decision impact | Notification (simplified) |
| **Class II** | Medium | Software providing info for diagnostic/therapeutic decisions (non-life-threatening) | Notification (simplified) |
| **Class III** | High | Software monitoring vital physiological parameters where errors could cause immediate patient danger | Full registration |
| **Class IV** | Maximum | Software for decisions where errors cause death or irreversible health deterioration | Full registration + BGMP |

([Saúde Digital Brasil](https://saudedigitalbrasil.com.br/anvisa-atualiza-regras-para-regularizacao-de-dispositivos-medicos-no-brasil/), [QServe Group](https://qservegroup.com/en/brazil-medical-device-regulations))

#### In-House SaMD Exemption

Low-risk SaMD (Classes I/II) developed in-house and used exclusively within the same health service (including branches) is **exempt from ANVISA registration**, provided:
- It does not interfere with registered hardware medical devices
- Validation records are maintained for ≥10 years after disposal
- Commercialization or donation without registration is **prohibited**
- Health services had a **2-year adaptation period** from July 1, 2022

([RDC 657/2022 English PDF](https://www.gov.br/anvisa/pt-br/assuntos/produtosparasaude/temas-em-destaque/arquivos/2024/rdc-657-2022-en.pdf))

#### Technical Dossier Requirements (by class)

- **All classes:** Description, intended purpose, risk management, conformity declaration
- **Classes II–IV:** Architecture, algorithms, cybersecurity plan, verification/validation, usability (IEC 62366), lifecycle documentation (IEC 62304)
- **Classes III–IV:** Clinical evidence via literature or clinical studies; BGMP certification required

#### Labeling Requirements for SaMD

- Primarily in Portuguese
- Must include: update procedures, hardware/software requirements, operating principles, interoperability specifications, cybersecurity environment description
- Electronic distribution acceptable; no physical labeling required for online-only distribution

([Elendiabs](https://www.elendilabs.com/en/articles/bra-software-as-a-medical-device), [Freyr Solutions](https://www.freyrsolutions.com/blog/resolution-for-regulation-of-software-as-medical-device-samd-in-brazil))

#### Non-Compliance Consequences

Administrative sanctions, including fines, suspension of sales, product seizure, and possible criminal/civil liability. ([CMS Law](https://cms.law/en/int/expert-guides/cms-expert-guide-to-digital-health-apps-and-telemedicine/brazil))

## 4. SUS Integration Requirements

### Background

SUS (Sistema Único de Saúde) serves ~72% of Brazilians (164 million people) and is one of the world's largest public health systems. ([ITA / Trade.gov](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-healthcare))

### RNDS — Rede Nacional de Dados em Saúde

**The RNDS is the national health data interoperability platform.** It was created in 2020 (Ministry of Health Ordinance GM/MS 1.434/2020) as a COVID-19 response measure and has since evolved into the structural backbone of Brazil's digital health system. ([DATASUS](https://datasus.saude.gov.br/modelo-padrao-de-dados-mad/), [Gov.br RNDS](https://www.gov.br/saude/pt-br/composicao/seidigi/rnds))

**Decree 12.560/2025** (July 2025) formally enshrined RNDS as the official interoperability infrastructure, mandating interoperability between public and private systems and establishing citizen data rights. ([Telesíntese](https://telesintese.com.br/decreto-regulamenta-rede-nacional-de-dados-em-saude-e-sus-digital/), [LinkedIn/Firely](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/unlocking-health-data-brazil-rnds-patient-rights-power-ward-weistra-nh2ze))

#### Technical Standard: HL7 FHIR R4

RNDS uses **HL7 FHIR R4** as the mandatory interoperability standard for all data exchanges. ([DATASUS](https://datasus.saude.gov.br/modelo-padrao-de-dados-mad/))

Brazil launched one of the world's largest national health interoperability platforms using FHIR in 2020. ([Wikipedia — FHIR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_Healthcare_Interoperability_Resources))

Prior to FHIR, early mandates (2011) required HL7 V2, CDA, and OpenEHR. ([LinkedIn/Firely](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/unlocking-health-data-brazil-rnds-patient-rights-power-ward-weistra-nh2ze))

#### Prerequisites for RNDS Integration

Establishments connecting to RNDS must:
1. Be registered in **CNES** (Cadastro Nacional de Estabelecimentos de Saúde — National Healthcare Facility Registry)
2. Hold a valid **ICP-Brasil Type A1 digital certificate** (e-CPF or e-CNPJ)
3. Complete **credentialing via DATASUS portal** (servicos-datasus.saude.gov.br)
4. Achieve **homologation** in the RNDS test environment before production go-live

([Voa Health Docs](https://docs.voa.health/integracao/rnds))

#### What RNDS Exchanges

Current RNDS data models include:
- Immunization records
- COVID-19 test results
- Hospital discharge summaries
- Medication dispensing records
- Clinical care records (in development)
- Prescription drug records (in development)

([Simplifier.net — FHIR Brazil](https://simplifier.net/jurisdictions/br))

#### Patient-Facing Access

Citizens can view their health data through the **Meu SUS Digital** app; professionals access it through **SUS Digital Profissional**. ([Gov.br RNDS](https://www.gov.br/saude/pt-br/composicao/seidigi/rnds))

#### SUS Digital Programs Driving Software Demand

- **APS Digital:** Provides IT equipment and software to basic care units across 3,613 municipalities (65% of Brazil). ([ITA / Trade.gov](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-healthcare))
- **UBS Digital:** USD 3M investment for telemedicine in remote areas (cardiology, dermatology, endocrinology, geriatrics). ([ITA / Trade.gov](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-healthcare))
- **Ministry of Health investment (2023):** USD 200M for SUS digitalization. ([ITA / Trade.gov](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-healthcare))
- **Ministry of Health investment (2024):** USD 84M in informatics for North and Northeast regions. ([ITA / Trade.gov](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-healthcare))

#### Key Challenge: Digital Divide

Nearly 40% of SUS primary care units lack stable broadband. Many facilities still use paper records or WhatsApp. Brazil's National Digital Health Strategy identifies this as a "new social determinant of health." ([Columbia Emerging Markets Review](https://www.columbiaemergingmarketsreview.com/p/public-health-meets-private-capital))

## 5. Telemedicine Regulation Evolution (Post-COVID)

### Pre-COVID Baseline

Brazil's telemedicine framework was minimal: CFM Resolution 1.643/2002 permitted telemedicine only for emergencies or when requested by the attending physician. CFM Resolution 2.227/2018 attempted broader regulation but was revoked amid controversy. ([IBA Net](https://www.ibanet.org/telemedicine-regulation-brazil-covid-pandemic))

### COVID-19 Emergency Authorization (2020)

**Ministry of Health Ordinance 467/2020** and **Law 13.989/2020** authorized teleconsultas exceptionally during the public health emergency. These provisions expired with the official end of the COVID emergency state. ([DLA Piper Intelligence](https://www.dlapiperintelligence.com/telehealth/countries/brazil/02-regulation-of-telehealth.html))

During the pandemic, telemedicine consultations increased by over **200%**. ([Research and Markets](https://www.researchandmarkets.com/reports/6209342/brazil-digital-healthcare-telemedicine-market))

### Post-COVID Permanent Framework

**Two parallel instruments** in 2022 created the current permanent framework:

#### CFM Resolution 2.314/2022 (physician-specific)
- Issued April 2022, published in Diário Oficial da União May 2022
- Covers physicians specifically; recognizes 7 telehealth modalities
- Sets detailed technical requirements (SRES, ICP-Brasil digital signatures, data security)
- Maintains in-person as the "gold standard" but allows virtual-first relationships
([AMB](https://amb.org.br/cfm-regulamenta-consultas-de-telemedicina-no-brasil/), [CMS Law](https://cms.law/en/int/expert-guides/cms-expert-guide-to-digital-health-apps-and-telemedicine/brazil))

#### Law 14.510/2022 (Telehealth Act — all health professions)
- Signed December 28, 2022; came into force immediately
- Permanently authorizes telehealth across **all** health professions governed by Professional Councils
- Defines telehealth broadly: remote service delivery using ICT, including texts, sounds, images
- Delegates ethics regulation to each profession's council (CFM for physicians, COFEN for nurses, etc.)
- Revokes Law 13.989/2020 (the COVID emergency authorization)
- Affirms LGPD compliance obligations for telehealth
([Souto Correa](https://www.soutocorrea.com.br/en/client-alerts/lei-que-regulamenta-a-telessaude-no-brasil-e-sancionada-e-entra-em-vigor-imediatamente/), [IBA Net](https://www.ibanet.org/telemedicine-regulation-brazil-covid-pandemic), [Demarest](https://www.demarest.com.br/en/law-that-regulates-telehealth-sector-sanctioned-by-the-president-of-brazil/))

### Market Impact

- **Doctoralia**: 3 million online consultations in 2024, +57% vs 2023. ([Doctoralia](https://pro.doctoralia.com.br/blog/clinicas/telemedicina-o-que-diz-a-lei-brasileira))
- Over **30 million telemedicine consultations** recorded in Brazil in 2024. ([LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/ash-frenken-86298042_brasil-vai-de-pa%C3%ADs-das-fintechs-ao-pa%C3%ADs-das-activity-7435870168297316352-44-A))
- Brazil E-Health & Telemedicine market valued at **USD 1.9 billion** (5-year historical analysis). ([Ken Research](https://www.kenresearch.com/brazil-e-health-telemedicine-market))
- The telemedicine market is projected to reach **USD 12.9 billion by 2034** at a CAGR of 20.52%. ([IMARC / LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/brazil-telemedicine-market-report-2026-2034-analyzing-kishan-kumar-opcoc))

### Ongoing Regulatory Development

- Ordinance 3.691/2024 formalized "Digital SUS Strategy Action — Telehealth" for the public system
- Pending legislation in Congress may further refine telehealth rules
- AI in telemedicine now addressed by CFM Resolution 2.454/2026
- Growth opportunities in: telecardiology, chronic disease management, remote monitoring, mental health teletherapy

## 8. Competitive Dynamics & Market Fragmentation

### Hospital & Insurer Market

Brazil's hospital market is **highly fragmented** at the small end but rapidly consolidating at the top:
- 7,309 hospitals total; 52% are located in municipalities and are small (<50 beds)
- Federal and state hospitals are larger and operate at higher complexity
- **Top consolidated players:** Hapvida NotreDame Intermédica (~18% of private insurance market, ~16M beneficiaries), Rede D'Or São Luiz (79 hospitals, 5.4M insured), Unimed (cooperative federation), Amil, SulAmérica (absorbed by Rede D'Or)

([Commonwealth Fund](https://www.commonwealthfund.org/international-health-policy-center/countries/brazil), [Matrix BCG](https://matrixbcg.com/blogs/competitors/hapvida))

The hospital-facing EHR/HIS market is dominated by a small number of players:

### Clinic/Outpatient Market Fragmentation

- Brazil has ~94% of healthtech startups with fewer than 50 employees (per Distrito data, 2020)
- The outpatient clinic space is highly fragmented; SaaS products like iClinic target the long tail of independent physicians and small clinics
- Major trends driving consolidation:
  - **RNDS integration requirement** creates pressure to upgrade systems
  - **TISS compliance** favors vendors with dedicated teams
  - **ANVISA SaMD registration** adds regulatory burden smaller players struggle to bear
  - **AI regulation** (CFM 2.454/2026) will require institutional AI governance that solo vendors cannot easily provide

### Key Market Structural Dynamics

1. **Two-tier system:** Private hospitals (63%) are well-funded, technology-forward, and adopt new solutions quickly; SUS facilities are underinvested and digital-divide-constrained.
2. **SUS is the market opportunity:** 164M people, government investment of USD 200M+ in 2023 alone — but procurement cycles are slow and pricing is compressed.
3. **Medical inflation (14.2% in 2025)** is forcing consolidation among small insurers and clinics, benefiting scale players.
4. **International capital entering:** Valsoft (Canada), Bain Capital, IFC — indicating attractive valuations and growth prospects for Brazil healthcare IT.
5. **AI differentiation emerging:** First-movers building ANVISA-compliant, CFM 2.454-ready AI governance frameworks will have a regulatory moat.

## 9. Summary Regulatory Checklist for Clinical Software

The following is a consolidated overview of what clinical software operating in Brazil must address:

| Regulation | Applies To | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **CFM 2.314/2022** | Telemedicine platforms; any software supporting remote care | SRES integration, ICP-Brasil digital signatures, patient consent capture, physician autonomy preserved |
| **CFM 2.454/2026** | Software incorporating AI for clinical decisions | Risk classification, AI committee, audit logs, physician override capability; effective Aug 10, 2026 |
| **LGPD (Law 13.709/2018)** | All software processing patient data | Data mapping, legal basis documentation, consent management, DPO, breach notification, encryption |
| **TISS (ANS RN 501)** | Software billing to private health insurers | XML-based electronic claims in TISS format; TUSS procedure codes; digital certificates |
| **TUSS** | All billing/coding with health insurers | Use ANS-maintained code tables; CBHPM-aligned |
| **ANVISA RDC 657/2022** | Software with diagnostic, therapeutic, or monitoring functions | Risk classification (I–IV), ANVISA notification or registration, technical dossier, cybersecurity plan |
| **ANVISA RDC 751/2022** | Medical devices and SaMD | Risk-based classification per 22 rules; Rule 11 for software |
| **RNDS / FHIR R4** | Software in SUS network or seeking interoperability | HL7 FHIR R4 integration; CNES registration; ICP-Brasil digital certificate; DATASUS credentialing |
| **CFM 1.821/2007** | All EHR/medical record systems | Patient records retained ≥20 years (physical) or permanently (digital) |
