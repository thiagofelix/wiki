Pixeon is one of Brazil's leading healthcare IT companies, offering an integrated portfolio covering the full spectrum of hospital, clinic, and diagnostic-center software: HIS (Hospital Information Systems), RIS (Radiology Information Systems), PACS (Picture Archiving and Communication Systems), LIS (Laboratory Information Systems), and CIS (Clinical Information Systems). Founded in Florianópolis in 2003, Pixeon reached its current form through a series of mergers and acquisitions, most significantly a ~2012 union with Medical Systems. In September 2025, it announced it would join **Vela LatAm** (a division of Canada's Vela Software), pending CADE approval. Pixeon is the only large-scale EHR in Brazil certified by SBIS at the highest digital maturity level, and its PACS Aurora product has been voted Best PACS in Latin America by KLAS Research four consecutive times. Among the platforms in the [[market-overview]], Pixeon occupies the enterprise hospital and diagnostic-center tier alongside [[mv-sistemas]], and differs fundamentally in target and product from clinic-focused SaaS platforms like [[iclinic]], [[shosp]], [[clinica-nas-nuvens]], and [[easy-health]].

## Target Market

**Hospitals, large clinics, diagnostic centers, and laboratories** — primarily medium-to-large institutions. Serves 3,000+ institutions across Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay, Colombia, and Peru. Key clients include Hapvida (triples imaging reports with Pixeon), LACEN-MG (853 municipalities), Hospital de Amor (oncology), and Hospital Ana Nery.

This enterprise focus mirrors [[mv-sistemas]] and contrasts sharply with clinic-focused platforms [[iclinic]], [[shosp]], [[clinica-nas-nuvens]], and [[easy-health]]. Pixeon's specific strength in PACS/RIS/LIS gives it a diagnostic-imaging niche that [[mv-sistemas]] also covers but through a different product architecture. See [[market-overview]] for segment comparison.

Source: [Pixeon Cases](https://www.pixeon.com/en/cases/)
