Brazil's telemedicine regulatory framework evolved from a restrictive 2002 baseline through COVID-19 emergency measures to a permanent, multi-layered framework in 2022, now supplemented by AI-specific rules taking effect in 2026. The governing body for physician-specific rules is the **CFM (Conselho Federal de Medicina)**, an autarchic federal body established by Law 3.268/1957. The broader telehealth framework for all health professions is established by federal legislation.

---

## Regulatory Timeline

| Year | Instrument | Key Change |
|---|---|---|
| **2002** | CFM Resolution 1.643/2002 | First telemedicine framework; limited to emergencies or at the attending physician's explicit request |
| **2018** | CFM Resolution 2.227/2018 | Attempted broader regulation covering teleconsulta, telecirurgia, telediagnóstico — later **revoked** amid professional controversy |
| **2020** | Ministry of Health Ordinance 467/2020 | Emergency authorization of telemedicine during COVID-19 pandemic |
| **2020** | Law 13.989/2020 | Emergency legal authorization; expired with end of COVID emergency state |
| **2022 (April)** | **CFM Resolution 2.314/2022** | Current permanent physician-specific telemedicine regulation; replaces all prior telemedicine rules |
| **2022 (December)** | **Law 14.510/2022** (Telehealth Act) | Federal law permanently authorizing telehealth across **all** health professions; revokes Law 13.989/2020 |
| **2022** | Ministry of Health Ordinance 1.348/2022 | Telehealth within SUS framework |
| **2024** | Ministry of Health Ordinance 3.691/2024 | "Digital SUS Strategy Action — Telehealth" |
| **2026 (February)** | **CFM Resolution 2.454/2026** | AI in medicine — enters force **August 10, 2026** |

## Pre-COVID Baseline (2002–2019)

CFM Resolution 1.643/2002 established a minimal framework that restricted telemedicine to emergency situations or cases where the attending physician explicitly requested a remote consultation. This created a de facto prohibition on routine teleconsultas ([IBA Net](https://www.ibanet.org/telemedicine-regulation-brazil-covid-pandemic)).

CFM Resolution 2.227/2018 attempted to modernize the rules — covering teleconsulta, telecirurgia, and telediagnóstico — but was revoked following backlash from the medical community over concerns about remote-only consultations and market disruption ([IBA Net](https://www.ibanet.org/telemedicine-regulation-brazil-covid-pandemic)).

---

## COVID-19 Emergency (2020)

**Ministry of Health Ordinance 467/2020** and **Law 13.989/2020** authorized teleconsultas exceptionally during the public health emergency. These provisions expired with the official end of the COVID emergency state ([DLA Piper Intelligence](https://www.dlapiperintelligence.com/telehealth/countries/brazil/02-regulation-of-telehealth.html)).

During the pandemic, telemedicine consultations increased by over **200%** ([Research and Markets](https://www.researchandmarkets.com/reports/6209342/brazil-digital-healthcare-telemedicine-market)).

## Permanent Framework (2022)

Two parallel instruments created the current permanent framework.

### CFM Resolution 2.314/2022 — Physician-Specific Rules

Published in Diário Oficial da União, May 2022. Covers physicians specifically and sets detailed technical platform requirements.

**Seven recognized telehealth modalities:**
1. Teleconsulta
2. Teleinterconsulta
3. Telediagnóstico
4. Telecirurgia
5. Telemonitoramento / Televigilância
6. Teletriagem
7. Teleconsultoria

([AMIB](https://amib.org.br/resoluc%CC%A7a%CC%83o-no-2-314-de-20-de-abril-de-2022/))

**Key requirements for software and platform providers:**

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Patient informed consent** | Must be captured before each telehealth encounter; includes consent to data transmission |
| **Electronic Health Record (SRES)** | Anamnesis, diagnostic data, and medical conduct must be recorded in the patient's SRES |
| **Security level** | System must support Level of Security Assurance 2 (NGS2) under ICP-Brasil or another legally accepted standard |
| **Digital signatures** | Remote prescriptions and reports require a physician's digital signature meeting ICP-Brasil standards |
| **Territorial requirement** | Service providers (companies) must be headquartered in Brazil and registered with the state CRM |
| **Physician autonomy** | Physician decides whether to use telemedicine; cannot be contractually removed |
| **Data storage and security** | Must follow CFM rules on storage, integrity, confidentiality, privacy, and professional secrecy |
| **Chronic disease follow-up** | Patients with chronic conditions must have an in-person visit at least every **180 days** |
| **Supervision** | Compliance enforced by the respective state CRM |

([CMS Law](https://cms.law/en/int/expert-guides/cms-expert-guide-to-digital-health-apps-and-telemedicine/brazil), [AMB](https://amb.org.br/cfm-regulamenta-consultas-de-telemedicina-no-brasil/))

### Law 14.510/2022 — Telehealth Act (All Professions)

Signed December 28, 2022; entered into force immediately.

- Permanently authorizes telehealth across **all** health professions governed by Professional Councils
- Defines telehealth broadly: remote service delivery using ICT, including texts, sounds, and images
- Delegates ethics regulation to each profession's council (CFM for physicians, COFEN for nurses, etc.)
- Revokes Law 13.989/2020 (the COVID emergency authorization)
- Affirms [[lgpd-health-data]] compliance obligations for all telehealth services

([Souto Correa](https://www.soutocorrea.com.br/en/client-alerts/lei-que-regulamenta-a-telessaude-no-brasil-e-sancionada-e-entra-em-vigor-imediatamente/), [Demarest](https://www.demarest.com.br/en/law-that-regulates-telehealth-sector-sanctioned-by-the-president-of-brazil/), [IBA Net](https://www.ibanet.org/telemedicine-regulation-brazil-covid-pandemic))

## AI in Medicine — CFM Resolution 2.454/2026

**Effective date: August 10, 2026** (180-day compliance window from publication date).

This resolution is the first Brazilian regulation specifically governing AI use in clinical practice.

### Key Provisions

| Provision | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Permitted use** | AI tools expressly permitted as **support instruments** for clinical decision-making, health management, research, and continuing education |
| **Mandatory human supervision** | AI cannot replace the physician's final authority over clinical decisions |
| **Continuous auditing** | Institutions must implement continuous auditing and monitoring of AI tools |
| **AI and Telemedicine Committee** | A dedicated committee under medical direction is required |
| **Risk classification** | AI tools must be classified as Low / Medium / High risk |
| **Transparent reporting** | Reporting requirements for AI use outcomes |
| **Physician right of refusal** | Any physician can decline to follow AI recommendations without penalty |
| **Documentation** | AI use in clinical decision-making must be recorded in the patient's medical record |

([JD Supra](https://www.jdsupra.com/legalnews/brazilian-cfm-issues-resolution-on-the-6731720/), [Machado Meyer](https://www.machadomeyer.com.br/pt/inteligencia-juridica/publicacoes-ij/life-sciences-e-saude/cfm-regulamenta-o-uso-de-inteligencia-artificial-na-medicina), [Medicina S/A](https://medicinasa.com.br/eval-ia-corporativa/))

The resolution creates three risk tiers that map implicitly to [[anvisa-samd]] SaMD classification requirements. Software vendors offering AI-assisted diagnostics or clinical decision support will need to implement governance frameworks before August 10, 2026.

## Summary: Software Compliance Checklist for Telemedicine Platforms

| Regulation | Requirement |
|---|---|
| CFM 2.314/2022 | SRES integration; ICP-Brasil NGS2 digital signatures; consent capture; physician autonomy controls; state CRM registration |
| Law 14.510/2022 | LGPD compliance documentation; applicable to all health professions using the platform |
| CFM 2.454/2026 (AI) | AI risk classification; AI & Telemedicine Committee governance; audit logs; physician override capability |
| [[lgpd-health-data]] | Explicit consent; DPO; breach notification; encryption |
| [[anvisa-samd]] | If software makes clinical decisions → ANVISA SaMD registration required |
