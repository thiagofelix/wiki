MV (formerly MV Informática / MV Sistemas, now operating as MV Saúde Digital) is Brazil's dominant hospital information system provider and one of the largest healthcare IT companies in Latin America. Founded in 1987 in Porto Alegre and re-headquartered in Recife, Pernambuco in 1988, MV built the first integrated hospital management software in Brazil and later introduced the country's first 100% web-based HIS (SOUL MV). As of 2025, it has positioned itself as the **5th largest hospital software provider in the world**, operates in 11 Latin American countries plus Africa, and announced a partnership to enter the US market. Among the platforms covered in the [[market-overview]], MV operates at the largest scale and institutional complexity — alongside [[pixeon]] in the enterprise tier — and targets a fundamentally different segment from clinic-focused tools like [[iclinic]], [[shosp]], [[clinica-nas-nuvens]], [[easy-health]], and [[doctoralia]].

## Target Market

**Large and medium hospitals, state health departments, public and private health networks, emergency units (UPAs), health plan operators (operadoras), diagnostic centers, and public health networks.** 4,000+ direct client users (up from 3,000+ previously) across Brazil and internationally. Platform impacts 77 million lives; 600,000+ professional users, including ~200,000 physicians.

Covers both public sector (SUS) and private sector hospitals — including the full national e-SUS public health digitization program alongside Philips Tasy. This dual public/private coverage is unique among the platforms in the [[market-overview]].

MV holds approximately **~40% market share** of HIMSS Level 6/7 certified hospitals in Brazil.

**Named clients:** Hospital Mãe de Deus, Município de Belo Horizonte, Unimed Sorocaba, Unimed Recife, HC USP, Santa Casa da Bahia, Hospital Unimed Recife III (HIMSS Level 7), Hospital Moinhos de Vento (HIMSS Level 7, with TechInPulse consultancy).

**International presence:** 11 Latin American countries (Argentina, Mexico, Uruguay, Peru, Ecuador, Panama, Dominican Republic, Guatemala, Colombia, and others) + Angola (first international client: Multiperfil Clinic, Luanda, Angola, 2005) + announced US market entry (September 2025 launch, announced October 29, 2025; Florida clinics targeted; products: MV Clinic and Global Health; presented at HIMSS Las Vegas, March 2025; Panama office). ([ABES, 2025](https://abes.org.br/en/mv-expande-atuacao-e-anuncia-entrada-no-mercado-dos-estados-unidos/)). Investment planned: ~US$10M via Medstation partnership.

## Pricing

Enterprise pricing — custom per institution. Not a self-service or per-seat consumer SaaS. The hubMV portal offers a 30-day free trial for smaller modules.

## Hospital Spotlights

### Hospital Unimed Recife III
Using MV since inauguration in 2011. Achieved HIMSS Level 6 in 2014, then HIMSS Level 7 on **December 1, 2016** — making it the first hospital in Latin America to reach Stage 7. At that time, only 9 hospitals outside the United States had achieved HIMSS Level 7 globally. Medical director: Fernando Cruz. ([Legismap](https://legismap.com.br/conteudos/artigos-e-noticias/primeiro-hospital-digital-da-america-latina-e-brasileiro))

### Hospital Moinhos de Vento
